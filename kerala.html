About Kerala

The State That Has Everything — Sea, Hills, Rivers & Culture

Kerala sits at the southwestern tip of India and packs more natural diversity into its 600km length than most countries manage in their entire territory. In a single week, you can wake up on a houseboat drifting through backwater canals lined with coconut palms, drive three hours to a misty hill station where the air smells of cardamom and tea, and end your day on a beach where the Arabian Sea turns silver at sunset.

The backwaters — particularly Alleppey and Kumarakom — are what makes Kerala genuinely unique in the world. There is nowhere else on Earth quite like them. A network of 900km of canals, rivers, and lakes connecting an entire coastal civilisation, navigated by traditional wooden kettuvallams (rice barges) converted into floating luxury hotels.

Kerala is also India's wellness capital — Ayurvedic massage, panchakarma treatments, yoga retreats, and meditation centres are world-class here. It's ideal for honeymooners who want romance and spa time, families who want nature and culture without adventure risks, and anyone who simply needs to decompress completely.

What to Do

Top 6 Experiences in Kerala

Every experience is organised and included in your Trip Harbour Kerala package

Houseboat Stay — Alleppey
A full day and night on a traditional kettuvallam, drifting through palm-fringed canals. Fresh fish cooked on board. A firefly night. Breakfast served as the mist lifts over the water. Unmissable.
Alleppey · Overnight
Munnar Tea Estate Walk
Walk through India's most beautiful tea gardens at dawn — cool mountain air, the smell of fresh-plucked leaves, and views over an endless carpet of emerald green. Visit the tea factory to see how it's made.
Munnar · 1,600m altitude
Periyar Wildlife Safari
Boat safari on Periyar Lake inside one of India's finest tiger reserves. Wild elephants coming to drink, sambar deer, bison, and over 260 species of birds. Best early morning or late afternoon.
Thekkady · 3 hrs from Munnar
Kovalam Beach & Ayurvedic Spa
Kerala's most famous beach crescent with warm Arabian Sea water, beachside Ayurvedic massage huts, fresh coconut sellers, and stunning sunsets. The Ayurvedic massage here is world-class.
Trivandrum · 16km
Fort Kochi Heritage Walk
Colonial-era streets with Portuguese churches, Dutch palaces, Jewish synagogue, and the iconic Chinese fishing nets on the waterfront. India's most European-feeling neighbourhood, with brilliant cafes and art galleries.
Ernakulam · Kochi city
Kathakali Performance
Watch the ancient Kerala classical dance drama with its elaborate face paint and costumes. Stories from the Mahabharata and Ramayana performed by masters. An authentic cultural experience no tourist should miss.
Available in Kochi & Thekkady

When to Go

Best Time to Visit Kerala — Month by Month

Kerala's monsoon (June–September) is heavy but also the best time for Ayurveda. Oct–March is peak tourism season.

January
Peak Season
Cool and dry. Best beach weather. Vibrant festivals. Highly recommended.
February
Peak Season
Excellent weather across all regions. Munnar cool and misty. Great for families.
March
Good
Getting warm. Good for backwaters. Last good month before summer.
April
Hot
Summer heat. Very humid. Not ideal for most travelers — only Munnar hill station is comfortable.
May
Pre-Monsoon
Hot and humid. Pre-monsoon rains begin. Not recommended unless going to Munnar.
June
Monsoon / Ayurveda
Southwest monsoon arrives. Heavy rains. But Kerala is stunning in green. Best for Ayurveda retreats.
July
Monsoon / Ayurveda
Peak monsoon. Waterfalls at maximum. Onam preparation begins. Great for Ayurveda packages.
August
Onam Season
Onam festival (10 days) — Kerala's biggest celebration with snake boat races. Unique cultural experience.
September
Monsoon Ending
Rains reducing. Lush and green everywhere. Fewer crowds, lower prices. Good value.
October
Season Opens
Post-monsoon freshness. Everything green and clean. Season begins. Ideal for all activities.
November
Ideal
Perfect weather. All attractions open. Beach season begins. Thrissur Pooram preparations.
December
Peak Season
Christmas in Fort Kochi — magical. Cool, dry, beautiful. Book early as hotels fill fast.
Travel Tips

Essential Kerala Travel Tips

Houseboat Tips
The houseboat experience is the centrepiece of any Kerala trip. Here's how to make the most of it:
Book a premium houseboat — the quality difference between budget and premium is significant
The best backwater routes are in Alleppey (Alappuzha) and Kumarakom
Houseboats stop at 5:30 PM — don't expect to cruise through the night
Fresh fish cooked on board is the highlight — tell your cook your dietary preferences in advance
Getting Around Kerala
Kerala is a long, thin state. Getting between regions takes time — plan your routing carefully.
Fly into Kochi — best connectivity. Fly out of Trivandrum if ending in the south
Kochi to Munnar is 130km — 3.5–4 hrs. Munnar to Thekkady is 85km — 3 hrs (winding mountain roads)
We arrange all transfers as part of your package — comfortable AC vehicles
Don't try to cover too much — Kerala rewards slow travel
Ayurveda — What to Expect
Kerala's Ayurveda is genuinely therapeutic, not just a spa. Here's what first-timers should know:
Authentic Ayurveda uses warm herbal oils — the massage technique feels different from Swedish massage
A one-time massage is good; a 5-day panchakarma programme is transformational
Monsoon (June–Sept) is considered the best time for Ayurveda treatments — pores are open
We book only certified Ayurvedic centres with qualified doctors
Food & Culture
Kerala food is one of the finest regional cuisines in India. Come hungry.
Kerala Sadya — a traditional feast on banana leaf with 20+ dishes — try it at least once
Karimeen Pollichathu (pearl spot fish) and Prawn Moilee are unmissable local specialties
Dress conservatively when visiting temples — remove footwear at entrance
Non-Hindus are not permitted inside some famous Kerala temples (like Padmanabhaswamy)
