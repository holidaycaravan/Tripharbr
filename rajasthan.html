About Rajasthan

Land of Kings, Forts & Desert Sunsets

Rajasthan is India's most visually spectacular state — a land of medieval forts rising from desert plains, ornate palaces reflected in man-made lakes, and a culture so vivid it feels like stepping into a painting. At 342,000 square kilometres, it's larger than Germany, and every city has a completely different personality.

The Pink City of Jaipur with its Amber Fort and bazaars, the Blue City of Jodhpur with its towering Mehrangarh, the Golden City of Jaisalmer with its living fort and sand dunes, and the romantic City of Lakes, Udaipur — each deserves days of exploration. Rajasthan rewards slow travel above all.

For Indian and international travelers alike, Rajasthan delivers a concentrated dose of the India everyone imagines — the camels, the forts, the textiles, the spices, the hospitality, and the colour. Top Highlights
Amber Fort, Jaipur
The magnificent 16th-century hilltop fort — a complex of palaces, halls, gardens, and temples built by Raja Man Singh. The Sheesh Mahal (Hall of Mirrors) alone is worth the journey.
Mehrangarh Fort, Jodhpur
One of India's largest forts, rising 125 metres above the blue-washed city. The museum inside is Rajasthan's finest — weaponry, paintings, and royal palanquins spanning 500 years.
Jaisalmer Fort
A UNESCO-listed living fort of golden sandstone — 3,000 people still live inside its walls. The havelis (merchant mansions) with their elaborate stone latticework are extraordinary.
Desert Camel Safari
Camel ride at sunset into the Sam Sand Dunes — an overnight luxury desert camp, folk music and dance around a bonfire, and the most spectacular stargazing in India.
Udaipur — City of Lakes
The most romantic city in India. Palaces and temples reflected in shimmering lakes, rooftop restaurants with views of the Lake Palace hotel, and the magnificent City Palace.
Pushkar Camel Fair
The world's largest camel fair (Nov) — 50,000 camels, cattle, and horses traded over 5 days, with cultural performances and the sacred Pushkar Lake at its centre.

Travel Tips

Essential Rajasthan Travel Tips

Best route
→Fly into Jaipur — start the circuit here. End in Udaipur — fly out from here. The Jaipur → Jodhpur → Jaisalmer → Udaipur route is the classic and still the best.
→Jaipur to Jodhpur is 340km — 5 hrs by road or 1.5 hrs by train. We arrange all inter-city transfers.
→Jaisalmer is the furthest point — consider a flight from Jodhpur to Jaisalmer to save the 5-hour drive.
What to wear
→Rajasthan is conservative — light, modest clothing is recommended, especially when visiting temples and palaces.
→The desert gets cold at night — even in October, temperatures can drop to 15°C after dark.
→Comfortable walking shoes are essential — fort courtyards and old city lanes involve a lot of walking.
→A dupatta or stole is useful to cover up at temples and to protect from sun and dust.
Shopping guide
→Jaipur: Johari Bazaar for jewellery, Bapu Bazaar for textiles, Blue Pottery Emporium for ceramics.
→Jodhpur: Clock Tower Market for spices, antiques, and the famous Rajasthani mojri shoes.
→Jaisalmer: Silver jewellery and Pashmina-style shawls inside the fort.
→Buy only from government-certified emporiums for Pashmina and gemstones — fakes are very common.
