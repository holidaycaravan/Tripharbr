About Kashmir
कश्मीर के बारे में

The Valley That Has Inspired Poets for Centuries

वह घाटी जिसने सदियों से कवियों को प्रेरित किया है

Kashmir is India's most romantically charged destination — and for good reason. The Kashmir Valley, cradled by the Himalayas, is a landscape of extraordinary beauty: still lakes reflecting snow-capped peaks, meadows carpeted in wildflowers, and a culture as rich and layered as the architecture of its ancient wooden mosques.

Srinagar is the heart of any Kashmir trip — a houseboat stay on Dal Lake is non-negotiable. Floating markets, shikaras gliding through lotus gardens, and the call to prayer echoing across the water at dawn — these are moments you don't find anywhere else in India. From Srinagar, Gulmarg (28km) and Pahalgam (96km) make superb day trips or overnight extensions.

Kashmir works beautifully for honeymooners who want scenery and romance, families who want a mix of culture and nature, and groups who want a destination that feels genuinely different. It's also one of the few Indian destinations that transforms completely with the seasons — green and flowery in spring, warm and golden in summer, richly autumnal in October, and fairy-tale snowy in winter.

कश्मीर भारत का सबसे रोमांटिक गंतव्य है। हिमालय से घिरी कश्मीर घाटी असाधारण सुंदरता का परिदृश्य है।

Top 6 Experiences in Kashmir

कश्मीर में शीर्ष 6 अनुभव

Every experience is included or arrangeable in your Tripharbr Kashmir package — no chasing vendors, no haggling

Shikara Ride — Dal Lake
डल झील पर शिकारा सवारी
The quintessential Kashmir experience. Glide through lotus gardens, floating markets, and bird sanctuaries as the morning mist lifts from the lake. Book the sunrise shikara — it's magical.
Srinagar · 1–2 hrs
Houseboat Stay — Dal Lake
डल झील पर हाउसबोट
Sleep in a traditional Kashmiri cedar-wood houseboat — ornately carved ceilings, antique furniture, and breakfast served while you watch the lake wake up. A once-in-a-lifetime stay.
Srinagar · Overnight
Gulmarg Gondola — Asia's Highest
गुलमर्ग गोंडोला
Asia's highest cable car takes you from Gulmarg (2,650m) to Apharwat Peak (3,980m). The panorama at the top — Nanga Parbat, K2, the Karakoram — is simply unmatched.
Gulmarg · 28km from Srinagar
Pahalgam — Betaab & Aru Valley
पहलगाम — बेताब और आरू घाटी
Pahalgam is Kashmir's Bollywood — virtually every classic Indian film has been shot here. Walk through Betaab Valley, horse-ride to Aru, and picnic by the Lidder River.
96km from Srinagar
Sonmarg — The Meadow of Gold
सोनमर्ग — सोने का मैदान
Named for its golden autumn colours, Sonmarg is a high-altitude valley at 2,740m surrounded by glaciers. Trek to Thajiwas Glacier — even children can manage the short pony ride.
80km from Srinagar
Kashmiri Cuisine & Wazwan
कश्मीरी व्यंजन और वज़वान
A traditional Wazwan feast is one of Kashmir's great gifts — 36 courses served on a giant platter. Rogan Josh, Yakhni, Gushtaba, Kahwa tea. We arrange an authentic Wazwan dinner in your package.
Srinagar restaurants

When to Go

Best Time to Visit Kashmir — Month by Month

Kashmir is a four-season destination. Each season offers a completely different and beautiful experience.

January
Snow Season
Heavy snowfall in Gulmarg. Perfect for skiing. Very cold in Srinagar (-5°C). Romantic for snow lovers.
February
Snow Season
Peak skiing at Gulmarg. Srinagar covered in snow. Fewer tourists than summer.
March
Spring Begins
Almond blossoms. Snow melting. Prices lower. Tulip Garden preparing to bloom.
April
Tulip Season
Asia's largest Tulip Garden in full bloom. Warm and colourful. Highly recommended.
May
Peak Season
Best weather. All attractions open. Sonmarg and Pahalgam stunning. Book early.
June
Peak Season
Warm and sunny. Dal Lake at its most beautiful. Excellent for families and couples.
July
Good
Monsoon touches Kashmir lightly. Occasional rain. Still very green and beautiful.
August
Good
Pleasant. Good for families. Lower crowds than May–June. Great value.
September
Best Month
Crisp and clear. Saffron harvest begins. Chinar trees turning amber. Absolutely stunning.
October
Autumn Magic
Chinar trees fully golden. Dal Lake misty. Our absolute favourite month for Kashmir.
November
Late Autumn
Getting cold. Beautiful colours still. Snow may begin in Gulmarg. Off-peak prices.
December
Early Winter
Snow in Gulmarg. Srinagar cold but peaceful. Great for couples who want a white Christmas in India.
Travel Tips

What You Need to Know Before You Go

Safety & Current Situation
Kashmir has been safe for tourists for several years. The tourism infrastructure is excellent and locals are very welcoming. That said, always check current advisories and travel with an organised operator.
Check Ministry of Tourism advisories before travel
Travel with a registered operator (like us) — they know the ground situation
Avoid venturing alone to remote areas without a local guide
Srinagar, Gulmarg, Pahalgam, Sonmarg are all very safe for tourists
What to Pack
Kashmir's temperatures vary significantly by season and altitude. Always layer.
Summer: light layers + a warm jacket for evenings and higher altitudes
Winter: heavy thermals, down jacket, snow boots — temperatures drop to -10°C
Comfortable walking shoes — Pahalgam and Sonmarg involve walking on uneven terrain
Rain cover or light waterproof jacket — even in summer, brief showers are common
Getting Around
We arrange all transport as part of your package. Here's what to know about Kashmir's transport:
Fly into Srinagar — 90-minute flight from Delhi, direct from most major cities
We arrange SUV or Innova Crysta for all your trips from Srinagar
Shikaras are hired by the hour — we include this in your package
Gulmarg Gondola tickets book out fast in peak season — we pre-book yours
Connectivity & Shopping
Srinagar has good connectivity. The shopping is extraordinary — Kashmir is world-famous for its crafts.
4G available in Srinagar, Gulmarg, Pahalgam. Limited connectivity in remote areas
Must-buy: Pashmina shawls (buy from government emporiums to avoid fakes)
Saffron, Kashmiri kahwa tea, walnut woodcarvings, papier-mâché — all authentic here
Bargaining is expected in local markets — your tour manager can guide you
