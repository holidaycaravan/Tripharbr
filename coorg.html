About Coorg

India's Coffee Country, Hiding in the Western Ghats

Coorg — officially known as Kodagu — is Karnataka's hill district, tucked into the Western Ghats at altitudes between 900m and 1,750m. The district is India's largest coffee-producing region, and the landscape shows it: rolling estates of Arabica and Robusta blanketed in shade trees, cardamom and pepper vines climbing between the coffee bushes, and the air carrying the faint sweetness of ripening fruit.

Madikeri is the district capital — a hill town with a 17th-century fort, a pleasant bazaar, and views across forested ridges from Raja's Seat, the old viewpoint where Kodava kings reportedly watched the sunset. From here, Coorg fans out into coffee country: Abbey Falls lies 8km away in a forest gully where a 70-foot cascade drops into a natural pool. Namdroling Monastery — the Golden Temple — is an extraordinary Tibetan Buddhist complex whose gilded interior is one of Karnataka's most unexpected and beautiful sights.

Coorg is ideal for anyone seeking a genuinely slow, restorative trip. The best experiences here aren't rushed attractions but unhurried rhythms — waking to mist over coffee rows, tasting fresh-picked beans, hiking a forest trail to a waterfall, sitting on a planter's veranda with a glass of homemade honey wine. It is also excellent for families and groups looking for a comfortable, accessible Indian hill destination with good roads and lovely properties.

Top Highlights
Madikeri & Raja's Seat — The district's hill capital, with the historic Madikeri Fort and the panoramic Raja's Seat viewpoint that glows at sunrise and sunset.
Abbey Falls Hike — A forested 500m walk brings you to a 70-foot cascade. The forest canopy closes overhead, the sound of the falls builds, and then suddenly — there it is.
Coffee Estate Walk & Tasting — Walk through working Arabica and Robusta estates with a planter's guide. Learn the bean-to-cup process, then taste the result — freshly roasted, freshly brewed.

Iruppu Falls & Brahmagiri Trek — A sacred waterfall in Brahmagiri Wildlife Sanctuary, leading to one of the finest one-day treks in Karnataka — the Brahmagiri peak trail.

What to Do

Top 6 Experiences in Coorg

Every experience is arranged and guided by Trip Harbour — so you spend your time in Coorg enjoying it, not planning it.

Coffee Estate Walk & Tasting
Walk through shaded Arabica and Robusta rows with a planter's guide. See the full journey from red cherry to roasted bean. End with a tasting session — fresh-brewed estate coffee in the morning mist is one of Coorg's finest moments.
Working Estates · 2–3 hrs
Abbey Falls
A forested walk leads to one of Karnataka's most photogenic waterfalls — a 70-foot cascade tumbling into a rocky pool below. The surrounding forest is alive with birdsong. Visit in the morning before the crowds arrive.
8km from Madikeri · 1–2 hrs
Raja's Seat Sunrise
Madikeri's famous viewpoint — the stone terrace where Kodava kings watched the sunset over the Ghats. At dawn, when mist fills the valleys between ridges and the sky turns pink above the coffee-covered hills, it's quietly extraordinary.
Madikeri Town · Sunrise
Namdroling Monastery
The Golden Temple — a Tibetan Buddhist monastery near Kushalnagar with three magnificent 20m gold statues of Buddha and Guru Rinpoche, surrounded by murals and intricate woodwork. Serene, colourful, and unlike anything else in Karnataka.
Kushalnagar · 30km from Madikeri
Iruppu Falls & Brahmagiri Trek
Iruppu Falls cascades 170 feet inside a wildlife sanctuary near Gonikoppal. The Brahmagiri peak trail from here is one of South India's finest — dense shola forests, bird-rich canopy, and panoramic Western Ghats views from the top.
Brahmagiri Wildlife Sanctuary
Coorg Cuisine Experience
Kodava food is one of India's best-kept culinary secrets. Pandi curry (slow-cooked pork in Coorg vinegar), akki roti, bamboo shoot curry, kadumbuttu rice dumplings. We arrange authentic Coorg meals at estate homestays and traditional restaurants.
Madikeri & homestays

When to Go

Best Time to Visit Coorg — Month by Month

Coorg is deeply affected by the Southwest monsoon from July to September — heavy, persistent rain makes travel difficult. October to February is the absolute sweet spot.

January
Best Month
Cool and dry. The coffee estates are post-harvest and peaceful. Misty mornings. Perfect weather for walking and sightseeing.
February
Best Month
Still cool and dry. Coffee blossoms begin to appear — the estates fill with white flowers and a honey-jasmine fragrance. Highly recommended.
March
Excellent
Warming up but not hot. Green and beautiful. Rivers full from the previous monsoon. Good for trekking and waterfall visits.
April
Good
Getting warmer. Pre-monsoon showers may begin toward end of April. Estate stays are pleasant; a few afternoon showers add atmosphere.
May
Good
Last month before monsoon. The hills are lush. Waterfalls filling. Manageable weather if you go in the first 2–3 weeks of May.
June
Monsoon Begins
Southwest monsoon arrives — typically very heavy rainfall. Roads can become difficult. Only for those who love watching the rain.
July
Heavy Monsoon
Peak monsoon — the heaviest rainfall month. Estates are impossibly green but outdoor activities are very limited. Avoid travel.
August
Heavy Monsoon
Monsoon continues heavily. Waterfalls are spectacular but dangerous. Many trails slippery and closed. Not recommended.
September
Late Monsoon
Rain begins to ease towards month end. The landscape is at its most intensely green. Travel in last week of September is possible.
October
Season Opens
Monsoon withdraws. Skies clear, temperatures perfect, waterfalls still full, estates lush. The best opening-of-season visit possible.
November
Peak Season
Coorg at its finest — coffee harvest season. Fresh-picked beans, golden estates, festive atmosphere. Book accommodation early.
December
Peak Season
Cool, crisp and beautiful. Peak holiday season — Christmas and New Year in a coffee estate is a wonderful experience.
Travel Tips

What You Need to Know Before You Go

Getting There
Coorg has no airport — the nearest are Mysuru (120km) and Mangalore (135km). Bangalore (270km) is the most common entry point with the widest flight connections.
Fly to Bangalore Kempegowda Airport, then road-transfer to Coorg (4–5 hours via NH 275)
Fly to Mysuru Airport (Air India / IndiGo) for a shorter drive — 2.5 hours to Madikeri
We arrange air-conditioned vehicle transfers as part of every package
Drive from Bangalore via Mysuru route — well-maintained NH 275, scenic final stretch
What to Pack
Coorg's hill climate is pleasant but variable. Even in summer, evenings can be cool at 900m–1,750m altitude. Pack in layers.
Light cottons for daytime with a fleece or light jacket for early mornings and evenings
Comfortable walking shoes for estate walks and waterfall trails — paths can be uneven and muddy
Insect repellent for forest walks — mildly necessary, especially near waterfalls and at dusk
A rain jacket even in dry season — Coorg can receive unexpected brief showers year-round
Where to Stay
Coorg's accommodation is one of its highlights — working estate resorts and planters' bungalows that put you in the middle of the coffee country rather than on its edge.
Stay on a working estate — we select properties where you can walk into the coffee rows from your room
Avoid town hotels unless you specifically need them — the estate experience is Coorg's best USP
Book early for October–February and all long weekends — popular properties fill fast
Many estates include guided estate walks and local meals — we factor this into your package
Shopping & Food
Coorg produces extraordinary things to take home. And Kodava cuisine is one of South India's most distinctive and delicious food cultures.
Buy directly from estate shops — fresh-roasted estate coffee, cardamom, pepper, and Coorg honey
Coorg plum wine and honey wine (sold by estates) make excellent gifts and are unique to the region
Must-eat: pandi curry (pork), akki roti, bamboo shoot curry, and kadumbuttu (rice dumplings)
4G connectivity is available in Madikeri and most estate areas; remote forest trails may have limited signal
