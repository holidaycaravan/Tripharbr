The Complete Rajasthan Travel Guide for Indians 2026

Forts, palaces, desert camps, and camel safaris — the complete guide to India's most visually spectacular state.

Quick AnswerRajasthan is best visited October through March. The classic 7-day circuit covers Jaipur, Jodhpur, Jaisalmer (with desert camp), and Udaipur. Fly into Jaipur and out of Udaipur. Budget ₹28,000–₹60,000 per person for 7 days including flights from Delhi. The desert camp at Sam Sand Dunes in Jaisalmer is non-negotiable — the most spectacular experience in Rajasthan.

Why Rajasthan is unlike any other Indian destination

Rajasthan is India at its most cinematic. Medieval forts rising from desert plains, lake palaces reflected in still water, camels walking through golden dunes at sunset, and a culture so vivid and proud that it permeates everything from the architecture to the textiles to the food. At 342,239 square kilometres — larger than Germany — Rajasthan is vast, and each city has a completely distinct personality. The Pink City of Jaipur, the Blue City of Jodhpur, the Golden City of Jaisalmer, and the City of Lakes Udaipur are four of India's most beautiful and distinctive urban environments, each deserving days of exploration.

When to visit Rajasthan

October–March (Best Season): Cool, dry, and perfect for sightseeing. Temperatures: 10–28°C. October and November are our top picks — post-monsoon freshness, fewer crowds than December peak, excellent visibility.

December–January: Peak season — busy, expensive, and beautifully cool (5–15°C). The Jaisalmer desert at night in December is extraordinary. Book well ahead.

February: Desert Festival in Jaisalmer — camel polo, folk music, turban-tying competitions. Outstanding cultural experience.

March: Holi in Rajasthan — celebrated with extraordinary colour especially in Jaipur and Pushkar. One of India's most vibrant Holi celebrations.

April–September: Avoid — extreme heat (45°C+ in the desert in May–June). July–August monsoon brings some rain to eastern Rajasthan.

How to reach Rajasthan

Jaipur: Fly from Delhi (45 mins), Mumbai (1.5 hrs), Bengaluru (2 hrs). Or overnight train from Delhi (5.5 hrs on Rajdhani).

Jodhpur: Fly from Delhi (1.5 hrs) or Mumbai (1.5 hrs). Train from Jaipur (5 hrs).

Jaisalmer: Fly from Delhi (1.5 hrs, limited flights) or overnight train from Jodhpur (5–6 hrs) — the train journey through the desert at night is atmospheric.

Udaipur: Fly from Delhi (1.5 hrs), Mumbai (1.5 hrs), Bengaluru (2 hrs).

Best circuit routing: Fly into Jaipur → road/rail to Jodhpur → Jaisalmer → road to Udaipur → fly out from Udaipur.

The classic Rajasthan circuit (7 days)

  • Day 1: Arrive Jaipur. Hawa Mahal, City Palace, Jantar Mantar.
  • Day 2: Amber Fort (half day — the most magnificent fort in Rajasthan), Nahargarh Fort sunset.
  • Day 3: Jaipur to Jodhpur (5 hrs by road or train). Evening at Mehrangarh Fort.
  • Day 4: Mehrangarh Fort museum (one of India's finest), Jaswant Thada, Clock Tower market.
  • Day 5: Jodhpur to Jaisalmer (5 hrs). Evening walk inside the living fort.
  • Day 6: Sam Sand Dunes — camel ride at sunset, desert camp dinner with folk music and dance under stars.
  • Day 7: Jaisalmer to Udaipur (8 hrs by road or fly). City Palace, Lake Pichola sunset.
  • Day 8 (optional): Udaipur second day — Saheliyon Ki Bari, Bagore Ki Haveli, rooftop dinner.

The unmissable Rajasthan experiences

Amber Fort Jaipur: The most magnificent fort in Rajasthan — a complex of palaces, halls, gardens, and temples built by Raja Man Singh in 1592. The Sheesh Mahal (Hall of Mirrors) is extraordinary. Allow 3 hours minimum.

Mehrangarh Fort Jodhpur: Rising 125 metres above the blue city — one of India's largest and finest forts. The museum inside is Rajasthan's best — weaponry, paintings, howdahs, and royal palanquins spanning 600 years.

Jaisalmer Fort: A UNESCO-listed living fort of golden sandstone — 3,000 people still live inside its walls. The havelis (merchant mansions) with their intricate stone latticework are extraordinary.

Sam Sand Dunes Desert Camp: The single most memorable Rajasthan experience. Camel ride at sunset over rolling dunes, BBQ dinner in a Bedouin-style camp, folk music and Kalbeliya dance around a bonfire, and the most spectacular stargazing in India. Non-negotiable.

Udaipur City Palace and Lake Pichola: The most romantic city in India. The City Palace complex overlooking the lake, the Lake Palace hotel on an island (visible from the lakeside — going inside requires staying there), rooftop restaurants with sunset views over the water.

What to eat in Rajasthan

Dal Baati Churma: Rajasthan's signature dish — baked wheat balls (baati) with five-lentil dal and sweet churma (crumbled wheat with ghee and jaggery). Extraordinary.

Laal Maas: A fiery Rajasthani mutton curry — one of India's most famous meat dishes.

Ker Sangri: A desert bean and dried berry preparation — uniquely Rajasthani and delicious.

Pyaaz Kachori: Jodhpur's famous onion-stuffed pastry — the best version in India is at Shri Mishrilal Hotel in Jodhpur Clock Tower area.

Mawa Kachori: Jodhpur's sweet version — filled with mawa (reduced milk) and dry fruits. Outstanding.

Ghevar: Rajasthan's disc-shaped sweet made with flour and ghee — particularly good in Jaipur during festivals.

Shopping in Rajasthan

Jaipur: Johari Bazaar for gold and silver jewellery, Bapu Bazaar for textiles and mojri shoes, Blue Pottery Emporium for the distinctive Jaipur blue pottery, Gem Palace for certified gemstones.

Jodhpur: Clock Tower market for spices, antiques, Jodhpuri mojri shoes, and handicrafts. Excellent antique furniture shops.

Jaisalmer: Silver jewellery and Pashmina-style shawls inside the fort. Camel leather goods.

Warning: Buy Pashmina and gemstones only from government-certified emporiums — fakes are extremely common in tourist markets.

How much does Rajasthan cost?

Budget per person, 7 days, excluding flights:

  • Budget: ₹14,000–₹20,000
  • Mid-range: ₹22,000–₹35,000
  • Luxury (heritage hotels): ₹50,000–₹2,00,000+
  • Flights from Delhi (Jaipur in, Udaipur out): ₹6,000–₹14,000
  • Total per person (mid-range): ₹30,000–₹50,000
Is Rajasthan Worth Visiting?

Rajasthan is India's most visually spectacular state — no other destination in the country delivers this combination of medieval forts, desert landscapes, living culture, and royal heritage in one circuit. The desert camp at Jaisalmer alone is worth the trip. Plan a minimum of 7 days, go between October and March, and let the colour and scale of Rajasthan do the rest.

Frequently Asked Questions

How many days are enough for Rajasthan?
7 days for the classic 4-city circuit. 10 days adds Pushkar, Ranthambore tiger safari, and Bundi. 5 days covers Jaipur and Jodhpur only — a good starting point if time is limited.
Which Rajasthan city should you visit first?
Start in Jaipur — it has the best airport connections, the most hotels, and the Amber Fort is an excellent introduction to Rajasthan's scale and grandeur.
Is the Jaisalmer desert camp worth the cost?
Absolutely — budget ₹4,000–₹8,000 per person for a good camp. The combination of camel ride at sunset, desert dinner, folk performance, and stargazing is the single best experience in Rajasthan.
Is Udaipur better than Jaipur?
Different in character. Jaipur is grander and more historically significant. Udaipur is more romantic and intimate. Both are essential — visiting Rajasthan without seeing Udaipur is a mistake.
What is the best way to travel between Rajasthan cities?
Private AC vehicle with driver — gives flexibility to stop at sights between cities and is the most comfortable option. Train is good for Jaipur-Jodhpur and Jodhpur-Jaisalmer. Flying saves time on longer segments.
