About Manali

The Gateway to the Himalayas

Manali sits at 2,050 metres in the Kullu Valley, cradled by the Beas River and surrounded by ranges that grow more dramatic the further you look. It is one of India's most beloved mountain destinations — and for good reason. Within a 30-kilometre radius of Manali town you can stand in snow even in June, paraglide over alpine meadows, raft through mountain rapids, and sit in an ancient temple surrounded by thousand-year-old deodar trees.

Rohtang Pass at 3,978 metres is the great drawcard — the gateway over the Pir Panjal range into the Lahaul and Spiti valleys, and eventually to Leh. But even travellers who never attempt the pass find more than enough in Manali itself: Solang Valley for adventure sports, Naggar Castle perched on a hillside above the Beas, Vashisht village for hot sulphur springs, and Old Manali's unhurried café culture and apple orchard lanes.

Manali works magnificently for honeymoon couples who want alpine romance, families who want a combination of snow, culture, and outdoor activity, and adventure travellers using it as a staging post for Spiti, Leh, or serious trekking. What to Do

Top 6 Experiences in Manali

Every experience is included or arrangeable in your Trip Harbour Manali package — no chasing vendors, no haggling

Rohtang Pass — Snow at 3,978m
Even in May and June, Rohtang Pass is blanketed in snow. The drive over the pass reveals a completely different world — barren, dramatic Lahaul on one side, lush Kullu on the other. Permits must be booked in advance — we handle this for you.
51km from Manali · Permit required
Solang Valley — Paragliding & Snow Sports
India's most accessible adventure valley. In winter it's a ski slope; in summer it's paragliding, zorbing, rope courses, and snowmobile rides on the remaining snow patches. Families and adventure travellers alike love it.
14km from Manali · All year
Hadimba Devi Temple
Built in 1553, this multi-tiered pagoda-style wooden temple is set within a forest of towering deodar trees. It is dedicated to Hadimba, the wife of Bhima from the Mahabharata. The silence and forest setting make it one of India's most atmospheric temple experiences.
3km from Manali · Any season
Beas River Rafting
The stretch from Pirdi to Jhiri offers Grade II–III rapids with spectacular Himalayan scenery. The river is full and fast from May to July when glacial melt is at its peak. A full rafting session takes 2–3 hours and is suitable for first-timers.
Pirdi–Jhiri stretch · May–July best
Old Manali — Cafés & Apple Orchards
The original village that predates the tourist town, Old Manali is a world of apple orchards, traditional stone houses, and cafés that have served travellers since the hippie trail era. Walk its lanes in the golden afternoon light, stop for apple cider, and watch the mountains turn pink at dusk.
2km from Manali bus stand
Naggar Castle & Roerich Museum
Perched on a hillside above the Beas River, Naggar Castle was built by the Raja of Kullu in the 15th century. Now a heritage hotel and museum, it also houses the Nicholas Roerich Art Gallery — the Russian mystic and painter lived here and created his most famous Himalayan canvases on these slopes.
22km south of Manali

When to Go

Best Time to Visit Manali — Month by Month

Manali is a year-round destination but each season offers a very different experience. Plan according to what you want.

January
Deep Winter
Heavy snowfall, Rohtang closed, roads icy. Only for experienced winter travellers. Manali town itself is accessible.
February
Deep Winter
Rohtang still closed. Solang Valley good for skiing. Very cold (-10°C nights). Not ideal for first-timers.
March
Early Spring
Snow thawing, roads reopening. Rohtang still closed. Cooler but pleasant. Apple orchards beginning to blossom.
April
Peak Season
Rohtang opens late April. Wildflowers blooming. Excellent weather (10–20°C). One of the best months to visit.
May
Peak Season
Best weather, all attractions open. Snow on Rohtang. Beas rafting excellent. Busy — book early.
June
Peak Season
Warm days, cool nights. Snow still on Rohtang. Green valleys. Crowded but wonderful. Book 2 months ahead.
July
Monsoon
Heavy rain, risk of landslides on Rohtang road. Manali town can be reached but excursions are difficult and risky.
August
Monsoon
Peak monsoon. Landslides common. Roads to Rohtang and Leh frequently blocked. Not recommended.
September
Best Month
Post-monsoon clarity. Crystal skies, fresh greenery, Rohtang open. Fewer crowds than May-June. Our favourite month.
October
Golden Autumn
Apple harvest season. Golden orchards. Crisp air. Rohtang open till mid-October. Spectacular colours.
November
Late Autumn
Getting cold. Rohtang closes for winter. Town is quiet and beautiful. Good for budget travellers who want solitude.
December
Winter Onset
Heavy snowfall begins. Rohtang closed. Very cold. Only for travellers specifically wanting a snowy Christmas getaway.
Travel Tips

What You Need to Know Before You Go

Rohtang Pass Permits
Rohtang Pass requires an online permit issued by the NGT (National Green Tribunal) to control daily vehicle numbers. This must be booked in advance and can sell out.
Book Rohtang permits online at least 7–10 days in advance in peak season
Only a limited number of vehicles are allowed daily — permits sell out fast in May and June
Trip Harbour books all Rohtang permits as part of your package — no queuing
The pass typically opens in April and closes in November; exact dates vary by year
What to Pack
Even in summer, Manali nights and high-altitude excursions require warm layers. Don't underestimate the cold at Rohtang Pass.
Carry a warm jacket and thermal base layers even if visiting in May or June
At Rohtang (3,978m), temperatures can be close to freezing even in summer
Waterproof boots or trekking shoes are essential for snow and muddy terrain
Sunscreen SPF 50+ — UV is intense at altitude and reflection off snow increases exposure
Getting to Manali
Bhuntar Airport (50km) has limited flights. Most travellers prefer Chandigarh (310km) and an overnight Volvo bus — or a scenic drive via NH3.
Chandigarh + overnight Volvo bus is the most comfortable and reliable route from Delhi
Bhuntar airport operates small aircraft — flights are often delayed or cancelled in bad weather
By road from Delhi is 530km — a long but scenic drive best done as a 2-day road trip
We arrange Volvo bus, private cab, or fly-and-drive packages — your choice
Acclimatisation & Altitude
Manali sits at 2,050m — mild altitude that most people handle easily. But Rohtang (3,978m) and Solang (2,480m) require a day of acclimatisation first.
Spend at least one full day in Manali before going to Rohtang — allow your body to adjust
Drink extra water and avoid alcohol on the first day at altitude
Headache and mild breathlessness are normal — rest resolves them; don't push through severe symptoms
Children and elderly travellers generally do well in Manali; Rohtang is fine with proper preparation
