About Manali

The Gateway to the Himalayas

Manali sits at 2,050m in the Kullu valley, where the Beas river cuts through dense pine forests and the snow-capped Himalayan peaks are visible from every corner of town. For travelers from the plains of North India, arriving in Manali feels like stepping into a completely different world — the air is cold and clean, the pace is slow, and the mountains are everywhere.

The road to Rohtang Pass at 3,978m is the centrepiece of every Manali trip — a high mountain pass on the watershed between the Beas and Chenab rivers, where snow remains almost year-round and the views stretch across the Himalayan range. Solang Valley offers a full day of adventure sports. The ancient Hadimba Temple in its cedar forest is one of the most atmospheric temples in North India. And Old Manali — the backpacker neighbourhood of cafes, Tibetan shops, and riverside walks — is one of the most charming spots in the Himalayas.

For travelers from Delhi, Manali is accessible by the famous 14-hour overnight Volvo bus — a journey that is itself an experience — or by flying into Bhuntar Airport (45 minutes from Manali). Snow-capped mountain pass with Himalayan panoramas. Snow available almost year-round. Gondola and snow activities at the top.

Manali's adventure hub 14km from town. Paragliding, zorbing, ropeway, and snow sports all available.
Hadimba Temple
A 16th-century wooden temple in a cedar forest. One of the most atmospheric temple settings in all of North India.

The charming backpacker neighbourhood with rooftop cafes, Tibetan handicraft shops, and Beas river views.

Grade 2-3 white water rafting through apple orchards and pine forests. Available April through June.

The 500-year-old former capital of the Kullu kingdom with panoramic valley views and the Roerich Art Gallery nearby.

Travel Tips

What You Need to Know Before You Go

Getting to Manali
Volvo bus from Delhi ISBT Kashmiri Gate — 14 hrs overnight, departs 5–6 PM, arrives 7–8 AM. Comfortable and scenic.
Fly to Bhuntar Airport, Kullu — connected to Delhi. 45-minute drive to Manali.
We arrange all airport and bus stand transfers as part of your package.
Rohtang Pass — What to Know
Rohtang Pass permits are limited daily and must be booked in advance. We arrange this as part of your package.
Snow is available at Rohtang almost year-round — even in summer (May-June) at the higher elevations.
The drive to Rohtang takes 2–3 hours from Manali. Start early — traffic builds up quickly after 9 AM.
Best Time for Snow vs Greenery
For deep snow and winter landscapes — December through February. Very cold (below 0°C at night) but extraordinary.
For the most comfortable weather with snow still at Rohtang — April through June.
For apple orchards, autumn colours, and clear skies — October. Our personal favourite month.
