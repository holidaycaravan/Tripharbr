About Dubai

A City That Shouldn't Exist

Dubai is a city that shouldn't exist — built from almost nothing in desert over 50 years into one of the world's most spectacular urban environments. The Burj Khalifa at 828 metres is the world's tallest building. The Palm Jumeirah is the world's largest artificial island. The Dubai Mall is one of the world's largest shopping destinations. Everything here is superlative.

For Indian travelers, Dubai is the most accessible international destination — 3 hours from Mumbai, 4 hours from Delhi, with dozens of daily flights on every major Indian airline. Indians form the largest expatriate community in Dubai, the city has outstanding Indian food, familiar vegetarian options everywhere, and a cultural comfort that makes it an easy and rewarding first international trip.

October through April is perfect — warm, dry, and ideal for all outdoor experiences Dubai offers. The desert safari is unmissable. The Burj Khalifa at sunset is a moment you'll carry forever. And the Gold Souk in old Dubai is one of the most extraordinary markets in the world. Trip Harbour handles visas, transfers, hotels, and every activity so your Dubai trip runs perfectly.

What to Do

Top 6 Highlights of Dubai

Every experience is included or arrangeable in your Trip Harbour Dubai package — no chasing vendors, no haggling

Burj Khalifa — At The Top
The world's tallest building at 828 metres. The observation deck on the 124th floor offers views of the desert, city, and Arabian Gulf simultaneously. One of the world's great viewpoints.
Desert Safari & Dune Bashing
The most unmissable Dubai experience. 4WD dune bashing at sunset, camel riding, BBQ dinner at a Bedouin camp, folk entertainment, and the most spectacular desert stargazing of your life.
Dubai Mall & Fountain Show
One of the world's largest shopping centres with an indoor aquarium, virtual reality park, and the Dubai Fountain performing every 30 minutes from 6 PM — the world's tallest choreographed fountain.
Palm Jumeirah
The world's largest artificial island shaped like a palm tree. Home to Atlantis The Palm hotel, luxury residences, and the best views of the Dubai skyline from the boardwalk.
Gold Souk & Spice Souk
Old Dubai's traditional markets in Deira. The Gold Souk has over 10 tonnes of gold on display. The Spice Souk just across the creek is fragrant with saffron, cardamom, and frankincense.
Creek Dhow Dinner Cruise
A traditional wooden dhow cruise on the historic Dubai Creek at night. The illuminated old city reflected in the water, with traditional abras passing by.

Travel Tips

What You Need to Know Before You Go

Visa for Indians
Indians get UAE visa on arrival at Dubai International Airport. Fee is USD 35 (approximately ₹3,000). Valid for 30 days.
Passport must be valid for at least 6 months from your travel date.
We provide a complete pre-departure checklist to all confirmed bookings.
Best Experiences — Don't Miss
Desert safari is the single best experience in Dubai — book the evening safari with dune bashing, not just the camel ride version.
Burj Khalifa — book At The Top tickets in advance online. Walk-in tickets cost significantly more.
The Dubai Fountain show outside Dubai Mall is free and runs every 30 minutes after 6 PM. Go twice.
Food & Shopping
Dubai has outstanding Indian food from Ravi Restaurant (legendary Pakistani cuisine) to high-end Indian fine dining at multiple Michelin-level restaurants.
Gold Souk gold is certified genuine — a great investment purchase. Bargaining is expected and accepted.
Tax-free shopping on electronics and perfume is genuinely excellent value compared to India.
