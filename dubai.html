About Dubai

The Desert That Became a Skyline

Dubai is perhaps the most audacious city on earth. In just five decades, it has transformed from a modest pearl-diving trading port into a glittering megalopolis of the tallest buildings, the largest malls, the most ambitious artificial islands, and some of the world's most extraordinary hospitality. For Indian travellers, Dubai is one of the closest international destinations — a direct 3-hour flight — and one of the most comprehensively rewarding.

The Burj Khalifa is only the most famous of Dubai's attractions. The city is divided into experiences that span the full spectrum: the utterly modern (Dubai Marina, Downtown Dubai, the Palm) and the richly traditional (the old spice and gold souks of Deira, the Dubai Creek abra ride, the Al Fahidi Historical Neighbourhood). A great Dubai itinerary balances both worlds — the spectacle and the soul.

Dubai works for everyone. Families will find Legoland, IMG Worlds of Adventure, and Ski Dubai at Mall of the Emirates. Couples will love the desert safari at sunset, Burj Al Arab afternoon tea, and dinner at the Dubai Frame. Corporate groups have world-class venue infrastructure, desert retreats, and a city that genuinely rewards the ambitious. And shoppers — Dubai is simply a different league.

Top Highlights
Burj Khalifa At the Top — The world's tallest building at 828m. The observation deck on floors 124 and 148 delivers views you will remember for the rest of your life.
Desert Safari & Dune Bashing — Sunset over the Arabian Desert is one of travel's great gifts. Dune bashing, camel riding, sandboarding, traditional dinner under the stars.
Dubai Mall & Fountain Show — The world's largest mall with an indoor aquarium, ice rink, and waterfall. The Dubai Fountain outside — the world's largest — performs every evening.
Gold & Spice Souks — The old heart of Dubai. The Gold Souk glitters with tonnes of jewellery. The Spice Souk fills the air with saffron, cardamom, and dried roses.
Palm Jumeirah & Atlantis — The iconic man-made island. The Palm monorail, Aquaventure Waterpark at Atlantis, and the views from the Palm Tower are extraordinary.
Ski Dubai — An indoor ski slope with real snow inside a mall in a desert. Five ski runs, a snow park, and a penguin encounter — genuinely surreal and brilliant.

What to Do

Top 6 Experiences in Dubai

Every experience is arranged in your Trip Harbour Dubai package — pre-booked tickets, desert transfers, and no haggling

Burj Khalifa — At the Top
The world's tallest building at 828 metres. At the Top observation deck on floors 124–148 gives you a view that stretches across the Arabian Gulf, the desert, and the gleaming Dubai skyline. The sunset view is the stuff of screensavers.
Downtown Dubai · 2 hrs
Desert Safari & Dune Bashing
One of the world's great travel experiences. An afternoon in the Arabian Desert — dune bashing in 4x4s, quad biking, camel rides, sandboarding at sunset, then a traditional Bedouin camp dinner under a sky full of stars. Magic.
Dubai Desert · Afternoon + Evening
Dubai Mall & Fountain Show
The Dubai Mall is a destination in itself — 1,200 shops, the Dubai Aquarium and Underwater Zoo, an indoor ice rink, KidZania, and a waterfall. Stay for the Dubai Fountain show at 6pm and 6:30pm — choreographed jets 150m high.
Downtown Dubai · Full day
Gold Souk & Spice Souk, Deira
The old Dubai — before the skyscrapers. The Gold Souk in Deira holds 10 tonnes of gold jewellery at any given moment. Nearby, the Spice Souk fills the air with cardamom, saffron, and turmeric. Take the abra across Dubai Creek.
Deira · 2–3 hrs
Palm Jumeirah & Atlantis
The iconic man-made palm-shaped island. Ride the Palm Monorail, gaze at the Atlantis resort, visit the Aquaventure Waterpark with its record-breaking slides. The view from the Palm Tower observation deck is staggeringly beautiful.
Palm Jumeirah · Full day
Dubai Frame — Past Meets Future
A 150m-tall golden picture frame that quite literally frames old Dubai on one side and modern Dubai on the other. The glass bridge walkway at the top, suspended 150m in the air, is terrifying in the best possible way.
Zabeel Park · 1–2 hrs

When to Go

Best Time to Visit Dubai — Month by Month

Dubai is at its best from October to May. June to August is extremely hot (45°C+) and most outdoor activities are unpleasant.

January
Best Weather
Peak winter. Cool and sunny at 20–24°C. Dubai Shopping Festival in full swing. Highly recommended.
February
Ideal
Perfect temperatures. Dubai Marathon and outdoor events. Shopping Festival continues.
March
Ideal
Warm and comfortable. Slightly humid. Dubai Food Festival. Best month for desert safaris.
April
Good
Getting warmer at 28–33°C. Still very manageable. Good deals on hotels before summer.
May
Acceptable
Hot (35–40°C) but manageable for indoor-heavy itineraries. Great hotel deals.
June
Very Hot
45°C+ and very humid. Outdoor activities unpleasant. Budget travellers only — prices are low.
July
Avoid
Extreme heat and humidity. Only for the very budget-conscious. Stick to malls and indoors.
August
Avoid
Hottest month. 45–48°C. Most locals leave for the summer. Not recommended.
September
Warming Up
Temperatures dropping from 40°C. Humidity decreasing. Good hotel deals before winter season.
October
Season Starts
GITEX Tech Week. Perfect temperatures returning. Events and outdoor activities resume.
November
Excellent

December
Best Month

Travel Tips

What You Need to Know Before You Go

Visa & Entry
Dubai requires a UAE tourist visa for Indian nationals. We handle the entire process — typically approved in 3–5 working days.
Apply 2–3 weeks before travel — we manage the full process
30-day single entry or 60-day multi-entry visa available
Your passport must be valid for at least 6 months
No vaccination certificate required for Indian nationals
Getting Around Dubai
Dubai is large. The Metro covers key attractions but a combination of Metro and taxis is most practical.
Dubai Metro (Red and Green lines) covers most major attractions
Careem (UAE's ride-app) is cheap and very reliable for short trips
We arrange daily transport as part of your package itinerary
Desert safari includes round-trip pickup from your hotel
Cultural Etiquette
Dubai is a Muslim country with a respectful attitude toward its culture. A few basics to know:
Dress modestly in public spaces — covered shoulders and knees
Swimwear is fine at hotel pools and beaches only
Public display of affection is frowned upon — be discreet
Alcohol is available in licensed restaurants and hotel bars only
Currency & Shopping
UAE Dirham (AED) is the currency. Dubai is almost entirely cashless — cards accepted everywhere.
1 AED ≈ ₹22–24. Exchange in India or at Dubai airport
Dubai Shopping Festival (Dec–Jan) offers incredible deals
Gold is cheaper in Dubai than India — but buy from reputable shops
Always bargain at the Gold and Spice Souk — it's expected
