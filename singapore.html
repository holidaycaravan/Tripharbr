About Singapore

The City That Reinvented Itself

Singapore is one of the world's most remarkable urban achievements — a tiny city-state of just 700 square kilometres that has grown into one of the most visited, most liveable, and most organised destinations on the planet. From a colonial trading port to a gleaming global hub in just six decades, Singapore's transformation is nothing short of extraordinary. And what makes it perfect for Indian travellers? Everything is close, everything works, and the food is extraordinary.

The famous Marina Bay skyline is just the beginning. Gardens by the Bay alone can consume an entire day — the Flower Dome, Cloud Forest, and the nightly Spectra light show at Marina Bay Sands are experiences that children and adults remember for years. Sentosa Island offers Universal Studios, SEA Aquarium, cable cars, and white sandy beaches — all within a few kilometres of the city centre.

What often surprises first-time visitors is Singapore's cultural richness. Little India pulses with the scent of jasmine and the sound of Tamil film songs. Chinatown dazzles with lanterns and heritage shophouses. Arab Street's Sultan Mosque is magnificent. Singapore rewards curious wanderers as much as thrill-seekers — and its hawker centres serve some of the world's most extraordinary food at prices that feel almost impossibly low for such a modern city.

Top Highlights
Marina Bay Sands SkyPark — The iconic infinity pool and observation deck 57 floors above the city. The view at sunset is absolutely unmissable.
Gardens by the Bay — 18 Supertrees, two stunning climate-controlled conservatories, and the breathtaking Spectra light and water show each night.
Universal Studios Sentosa — Southeast Asia's only Universal Studios with world-class rides and themed zones. A full day for families and thrill-seekers.
Hawker Centre Food Trail — Maxwell, Lau Pa Sat, Newton Circus. Chilli crab, Hainanese chicken rice, laksa — some of the world's best food for under ₹300.
Night Safari — The world's first nocturnal zoo. Tram rides through jungles lit only by moonlight, watching animals in their natural night-time behaviour.
Little India & Chinatown — Cultural neighbourhoods that feel like a completely different world. Temple visits, street food, shopping, and stunning heritage architecture.

Best Time to Visit Singapore — Month by Month

Singapore is a true year-round destination with warm temperatures and manageable rain throughout the year. December and January are slightly cooler and festive.

Travel Tips

What You Need to Know Before You Go

Visa for Indian Nationals
Singapore requires a visa for Indian passport holders. We handle the entire process — you just share your documents.
Apply 3–4 weeks before your travel date
Singapore visa is typically approved within 3–5 working days
We assist with the complete application as part of your package
Valid for 30 days, single or multiple entry available
Getting Around — MRT is Easy
Singapore's MRT (Mass Rapid Transit) is one of the world's best metro systems — clean, air-conditioned, cheap, and punctual.
Get an EZ-Link card at the airport for seamless MRT and bus travel
Grab (like Ola) is cheap and reliable for short trips
Sentosa has its own monorail and cable car connections
We arrange airport transfers — no confusion on arrival day
Food, Cash & Connectivity
Singapore is largely cashless but hawker centres prefer cash. 4G connectivity is excellent throughout.
Hawker centres: carry Singapore dollars — many stalls don't take cards
SGD exchange is best done in India before departure
Pick up a local SIM at Changi Airport — very affordable
Changi Airport itself is worth 2–3 hours — waterfall, cinema, gardens
What to Wear & Rules
Singapore is hot and humid. Dress light but carry a light layer for heavily air-conditioned malls and attractions.
Light cotton or linen — it's 30°C and humid year-round
Comfortable walking shoes — you will walk several kilometres daily
Singapore has strict rules — no littering, no chewing gum, no jaywalking
Modest dress required at mosques and some temples
