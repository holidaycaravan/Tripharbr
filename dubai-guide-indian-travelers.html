Dubai for Indian Travelers — The Complete Honest Guide 2026

Everything Indian travelers need to know about Dubai — from visa on arrival to the best Indian restaurants, written honestly without tourism board fluff.

Quick AnswerIndians need a visa on arrival for Dubai (USD 35, 30 days). Best time: October through April. Fly from Delhi (4 hrs) or Mumbai (3 hrs). Stay 4–5 days covering Burj Khalifa, desert safari, Gold Souk, and Dubai Mall. Budget ₹55,000–₹1,20,000 per couple for 5 days including flights. Dubai has outstanding Indian food, familiar vegetarian options, and a comfort level that makes it an easy first international destination.

Why Dubai works so well for Indian travelers

Dubai has the largest Indian expatriate community in the world — over 3 million Indians live and work in the UAE. This means the city is exceptionally well-calibrated for Indian visitors. Outstanding Indian restaurants at every price point, vegetarian options on every menu, Hindi spoken widely in shops and markets, and a cultural familiarity that makes Dubai feel both foreign and welcoming simultaneously. Add a 3-hour flight from Mumbai, visa on arrival, and some of the world’s most spectacular architecture — and Dubai makes compelling sense as an international destination for Indian travelers.

Visa and entry for Indians

Visa on Arrival at Dubai International Airport (DXB). Fee: USD 35 per person (approximately ₹2,900). Valid 30 days. Requirements: Valid Indian passport (6 months validity from entry), confirmed return ticket, proof of accommodation. Process: 15–30 minutes at the airport. Pay by international card or USD cash. Note: Not all Indian passport holders qualify automatically — check current UAE visa policy before travel, as rules occasionally change.

When to visit Dubai

October–April (Best Season): Temperatures of 20–30°C. All outdoor experiences enjoyable. December and January are peak — cooler temperatures (18–24°C) and Dubai Shopping Festival. February: Dubai Food Festival. March: Ideal weather for outdoor exploration.

May–September (Extreme Heat): Temperatures exceed 40°C regularly — up to 48°C in July–August. All outdoor activities are uncomfortable. Indoor attractions (malls, museums, indoor theme parks) are heavily air-conditioned and perfectly enjoyable — some travelers specifically visit in summer for the lowest hotel prices. Not recommended for first-time visitors.

How to reach Dubai

By Air: Dubai International Airport (DXB) — one of the world's busiest airports. Direct flights from Mumbai (3 hrs), Delhi (4 hrs), Chennai (4 hrs), Bengaluru (4 hrs), Kochi (3.5 hrs), Hyderabad (4 hrs), Kolkata (5 hrs). Airlines: IndiGo, Air India, Emirates, Vistara, flydubai, SpiceJet. Dozens of daily departures from all major Indian cities. Rarely needs booking more than 3–4 weeks ahead except during December peak.

The unmissable Dubai experiences

Burj Khalifa At The Top: The world's tallest building at 828m. The 124th floor observation deck offers views of the desert, the city, the Palm Jumeirah, and the Arabian Gulf simultaneously. Book online — walk-in tickets cost 30–40% more. AED 149–249 (₹3,400–₹5,700). The 148th floor (At The Top Sky) is the highest accessible point at AED 379.

Desert Safari: The single best Dubai experience. Evening dune bashing in a 4WD (adrenaline-filled ride over sand dunes), camel riding, henna, falconry demonstration, BBQ dinner with unlimited food and soft drinks, Arabic coffee and dates, belly dance and Tanoura performance, and stargazing in the desert. Cost: AED 200–350 per person (₹4,500–₹8,000). Always book through a licensed operator.

Dubai Mall and Dubai Fountain: One of the world's largest malls with an indoor aquarium (AED 130/₹3,000 per person), virtual reality park, and the Dubai Fountain performing every 30 minutes from 6 PM — the world's tallest choreographed water fountain. Entry to the mall and fountain show: FREE.

Gold Souk and Spice Souk: Old Dubai's traditional markets in Deira. The Gold Souk has over 10 tonnes of gold on display in 300+ shops. The Spice Souk across the Creek is fragrant with cardamom, saffron, and frankincense. Take an abra (traditional wooden ferry) across the Creek — AED 1 per person (₹23).

Palm Jumeirah: The world's largest artificial palm-shaped island. Atlantis The Palm hotel water park is outstanding (AED 350+/₹8,000 per person). The Palm Monorail gives views of the island.

Dubai Frame: A massive picture frame-shaped building bridging old and new Dubai — extraordinary views from the glass floor at 150m height. AED 50 (₹1,150) per person.

Indian food in Dubai

Dubai has some of the finest Indian food outside India.

Budget: Ravi Restaurant in Satwa — legendary Pakistani-Indian dhaba, open 24 hours, meals ₹300–₹500 per person. Al Ustad Special Kabab in Deira — outstanding kebabs.

Mid-range: Punjab Grill, Gazebo, Saravana Bhavan (South Indian, multiple locations).

Fine dining: Rang Mahal by Atul Kochhar, Indego by Vineet — Michelin-inspired Indian fine dining.

Vegetarian: Completely manageable — most Indian restaurants have extensive vegetarian menus. Saravana Bhavan is entirely vegetarian. Govinda’s (ISKCON) serves pure vegetarian food.

Getting around Dubai

Dubai Metro: Clean, air-conditioned, and connects most major attractions on the Red and Green lines. A NOL card (like a metro card) costs AED 25 and is rechargeable. Very affordable.

Uber/Careem: Reliable, metered, and well-priced. Use the app — never unlicensed taxis.

Dubai Taxis: Metered and reliable. More expensive than Careem but available everywhere.

Note: Dubai is spread over a large area — distances between attractions can be significant. Factor transport time into your planning.

How much does Dubai cost for Indians?

Budget per couple, 5 days:

  • Flights (return): ₹25,000–₹40,000
  • Hotel (4 nights, 4-star): ₹40,000–₹65,000
  • Visa (2 people): ₹5,800
  • Burj Khalifa + Desert Safari: ₹17,000–₹25,000
  • Food (5 days): ₹12,000–₹22,000
  • Transport: ₹6,000–₹10,000
  • Total per couple: ₹1,05,800–₹1,67,800
Is Dubai Worth Visiting for Indians?

Dubai is a genuinely remarkable city — the Burj Khalifa's scale, the desert under a billion stars, the Gold Souk, and the sheer ambition of what has been built in 50 years are impressive in a way that photographs do not capture. For Indian travelers specifically, the familiarity of the Indian community, the excellent Indian food, and the short flight make it an easy first international experience. Go once — it is worth seeing.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Dubai suitable for vegetarians?
Yes — Dubai has excellent vegetarian options particularly in the Indian restaurant belt of Karama, Deira, and Bur Dubai. Saravana Bhavan has multiple branches. Most restaurants clearly mark vegetarian options.
How many days are enough for Dubai?
4–5 days covers Burj Khalifa, desert safari, Dubai Mall, Gold Souk, dhow cruise, and optionally Abu Dhabi (2 hours from Dubai).
Is Dubai expensive compared to Singapore?
Comparable overall. Dubai hotels are slightly cheaper in mid-range. Singapore transport is cheaper. Dubai has more free outdoor experiences (the fountain show, the souks, the Creek walk).
Can Indian women travel safely to Dubai?
Yes — Dubai is one of the world's safest cities for women including solo travelers. Dress modestly in traditional areas (the souks, mosques). In malls, resorts, and tourist areas, normal attire is fine.
What is the best area to stay in Dubai?
Downtown Dubai for proximity to Burj Khalifa and Dubai Mall. Deira for budget travelers and Gold Souk access. JBR (Jumeirah Beach Residence) for beach access and Marina views.
