About Shimla

The Summer Capital That Never Lost Its Charm

Shimla sits along a 12-kilometre ridge at 2,276 metres, draped in cedar and pine, and it carries its history lightly. Built by the British as the summer capital of the Raj, the town still moves at a promenade pace: the traffic-free Mall Road, the Gothic spires of Christ Church on the Ridge, and the gabled Viceregal Lodge where India's independence was negotiated.

The journey is half the destination. The UNESCO-listed Kalka–Shimla toy train climbs 96 kilometres through 102 tunnels and over 800 bridges, a five-hour ride through pine forests and hill villages that children and grandparents love equally. Once in town, Kufri (16km) offers snow play and yak rides in winter, while Mashobra and Naldehra's cedar glades and golf course make quiet half-day escapes.

Shimla works beautifully as a first hill station — close enough to Delhi and Chandigarh for a long weekend, gentle enough in altitude for all ages, and rich enough in walks, views, and heritage to fill five days. Trip Harbour handles hotels, cabs, toy train bookings, and a dedicated tour manager so all you do is stroll the Mall.

What to Do

Top 6 Experiences in Shimla

Every experience is included or arrangeable in your Trip Harbour Shimla package — no chasing vendors, no haggling

Walk the Ridge & Mall Road
Shimla’s heart is entirely pedestrian. Start at Scandal Point, take in Christ Church’s stained glass, browse the colonial-era bookshops, and end with sunset from the Ridge as lights come on across the valley below.
Town centre · All year
Ride the Kalka–Shimla Toy Train
Board at Kalka and climb for five unhurried hours through 102 tunnels, over stone-arch bridges, past pine forests and tiny hill stations. Book the glass-roofed Him Darshan coach for the full panorama.
UNESCO Heritage · Book ahead
Snow Day at Kufri
From December to February, Kufri turns into Shimla’s snow playground — sledging, skiing lessons, snowman territory. In summer it swaps snow for horse treks to Mahasu Peak’s 270° Himalayan viewpoint.
16km from Shimla · Dec–Feb for snow
Tour the Viceregal Lodge
Wander the manicured lawns and wood-panelled halls of the Indian Institute of Advanced Study — formerly the Viceroy’s summer residence, and the single most impressive colonial building in the Himalayas.
Observatory Hill · Guided tours daily
Climb to Jakhoo Temple
A steep 30-minute forest walk (or a short cable-car ride) brings you to Shimla’s highest point, its ancient Hanuman temple, and the 108-foot statue that watches over the town. Beware the famously bold monkeys.
2,455m · Cable car available
Day Trip to Naldehra & Mashobra
Escape to cedar glades 22km from town: Naldehra’s 9-hole golf course laid out in 1905, orchard lunches in Mashobra, and pine-forest walks with barely another tourist in sight.
22km from Shimla · Half day

When to Go

Best Time to Visit Shimla — Month by Month

Shimla is a true year-round hill station — snow in winter, cool escapes in summer, and quiet gold in autumn.

January
Snow Season
Snowfall in town and heavy snow at Kufri. Magical but cold (-2°C nights). Perfect for snow-seekers and honeymooners.
February
Late Winter
Snow lingers at Kufri. Crisp sunny days. Quieter than December-January. Great value on hotels.
March
Spring
Rhododendrons bloom, snow melts, walking weather returns. One of the loveliest and least crowded months.
April
Peak Season
Warm days (20°C), cool evenings. All excursions open. Delhi and Chandigarh crowds begin on weekends.
May
Peak Season
The classic summer-escape month. Book hotels 4–6 weeks ahead. Mornings are best for Kufri and Jakhoo.
June
Peak Season
Warmest month, still pleasant at altitude. Very busy until schools reopen late June.
July
Monsoon
Frequent rain and occasional landslides on approach roads. Misty charm for romantics, but excursions are hit-and-miss.
August
Monsoon
Peak monsoon. Roads and views unreliable. Only for travellers who enjoy the mist and low-season quiet.
September
Post-Monsoon
Skies clear, hills freshly green, crowds thin. An underrated month for walkers and photographers.
October
Golden Autumn
Crystal visibility, crisp air, apple harvest in surrounding orchards. Superb month all round.
November
Late Autumn
Cold sets in, skies stay clear. Quiet, atmospheric, and good hotel rates before the Christmas rush.
December
Christmas Season
First snowfalls, festive lights on Mall Road, peak romance. Christmas–New Year week books out months ahead.
Travel Tips

What You Need to Know Before You Go

Book the Toy Train Early
The Kalka–Shimla toy train is hugely popular and seats — especially the glass-roofed Him Darshan Express — sell out weeks ahead in season.
Reserve toy train tickets 30–60 days ahead for April–June and December travel
The full ride takes about 5 hours — the morning departures have the best light
Trip Harbour books your toy train seats as part of every Shimla package
Prone to motion sickness? The train is gentle, but sit facing forward in the front coaches
What to Pack
Shimla weather swings with altitude and season — layers always win.
A warm jacket is needed year-round for evenings; heavy woollens December–February
Comfortable walking shoes are essential — Shimla is a town of slopes and staircases
Sunscreen and sunglasses — the mountain sun is stronger than it feels
An umbrella or rain shell in monsoon and for surprise winter drizzle
Getting to Shimla
Chandigarh is the main gateway; the final climb is by road or the famous narrow-gauge rail.
Chandigarh airport (110km) has the best flight connections — 3.5 hours by road to Shimla
The toy train from Kalka is the scenic route — combine cab-to-Kalka + train for the best of both
Direct Volvo buses run overnight from Delhi (7–8 hours) — comfortable and economical
Cars cannot enter the Mall Road area — hotels arrange porters for luggage from the drop point
Beat the Crowds
Shimla is the most accessible hill station in North India — timing is everything.
Visit midweek if you can — weekend crowds from Delhi/Chandigarh triple the footfall
Do Kufri before 10am to beat the day-trip rush and get clean snow in winter
March, September, and November offer 80% of the experience with 20% of the crowds
Book December 20 – January 2 stays at least two months in advance
