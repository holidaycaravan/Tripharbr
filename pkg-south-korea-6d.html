Day-by-Day Itinerary
Day
1
Arrive Seoul
Incheon Airport · Myeongdong · Cheonggyecheon Stream
▼
Arrive Incheon International Airport — one of the world's best. Transfer to your hotel in central Seoul. Evening in Myeongdong — Seoul's most famous shopping and street food district: Korean BBQ, tteokbokki, and kimbap on every corner. Stroll the illuminated Cheonggyecheon Stream.
Airport TransferHotel Check-inMyeongdong Street FoodCheonggyecheon Stream
Day
2
Seoul Palaces + Hanbok
Gyeongbokgung · Hanbok rental · Bukchon · N Seoul Tower
▼
Morning at Gyeongbokgung Palace — the grand 14th-century Joseon dynasty palace — with optional hanbok (traditional Korean dress) rental for palace photos. Afternoon walk through Bukchon Hanok Village, a preserved neighbourhood of 600-year-old tile-roof homes. Evening cable car to N Seoul Tower on Namsan Mountain for the city panorama.
Gyeongbokgung PalaceHanbok RentalBukchon Hanok VillageN Seoul TowerBreakfast
Day
3
DMZ Tour
Imjingak · 3rd Infiltration Tunnel · Dora Observatory · JSA
▼
The most sobering day of the trip. A guided DMZ tour to the Demilitarised Zone dividing the two Koreas: Imjingak Peace Park, the 3rd Infiltration Tunnel dug by North Korea, Dora Observatory overlooking North Korean territory, and the Joint Security Area at Panmunjom — the blue huts straddling the Military Demarcation Line.
DMZ Imjingak3rd Infiltration TunnelDora ObservatoryJSA PanmunjomBreakfast
Day
4
Seoul → Gyeongju by KTX
K-Food cooking class · KTX bullet train · Tumuli Park tombs
▼
Morning K-Food cooking class — learn to make bibimbap, japchae, and doenjang jjigae. Afternoon KTX bullet train to Gyeongju (2 hours). Gyeongju was the capital of the Silla Kingdom for 1,000 years: Tumuli Park's royal burial mounds, Cheomseongdae observatory (7th century AD, Asia's oldest), and the illuminated Anapji Pond at night.
K-Food Cooking ClassSeoul–Gyeongju KTXTumuli ParkCheomseongdae ObservatoryAnapji PondBreakfast
Day
5
Gyeongju → Busan — Seafood City
Bulguksa Temple · Seokguram Grotto · Gamcheon Culture Village
▼
UNESCO Bulguksa Temple — the pinnacle of Silla Buddhist architecture — and the Seokguram stone grotto Buddha (8th century). Drive to Busan (1 hour): Gamcheon Culture Village, a hillside shantytown transformed into a psychedelic open-air gallery of murals and art. Haeundae Beach sunset and a Jagalchi seafood market dinner.
Bulguksa Temple (UNESCO)Seokguram GrottoGamcheon Culture VillageHaeundae BeachBreakfast + Seafood Dinner
Day
6
Busan Departure
Beach morning · Gimhae Airport · Fly home
▼
Final morning at Haeundae Beach or the Busan Museum of Art. Transfer to Gimhae Airport for your flight home, carrying kimchi paste, Korean skincare products, and a deep admiration for one of Asia's most compelling cultures.
