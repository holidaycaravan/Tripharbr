About Andaman Islands

India's Secret Paradise — No Passport Needed

The Andaman Islands sit in the Bay of Bengal, 1,400km from the Indian mainland, but only 2-3 hours from most South Indian cities by air. And here, within India, you'll find beaches that rival Thailand and the Maldives — turquoise water, white sand, and coral reefs teeming with life.

Havelock Island's Radhanagar Beach was ranked Asia's best beach by Time magazine. The waters around Neil Island have coral gardens with sea turtles, reef sharks, and hundreds of fish species visible without scuba equipment. And yet most Indian travelers have never been — which means the islands are still uncrowded and genuinely wild.

The Andamans are the best-kept secret for Indian honeymooners and beach lovers. No passport, no visa, prices a fraction of the Maldives, and beaches just as beautiful. Tripharbr handles the ferry bookings, island logistics, and everything in between — the most complex part of planning an Andaman trip.

Top Highlights
Radhanagar Beach
Ranked Asia's best beach by Time magazine. A 2km arc of pristine white sand with calm turquoise water. The sunset here is among the most beautiful in India.
Elephant Beach Snorkelling
The best snorkelling in the Andamans — crystal clear water with 15m visibility, coral gardens, sea turtles, and reef sharks accessible from the beach.
Cellular Jail, Port Blair
The iconic colonial prison where India's freedom fighters were incarcerated — a deeply moving Sound and Light show narrates the independence struggle.
Neil Island — Natural Bridge
The quietest and most beautiful of the Andaman islands. The Natural Rock Bridge over the sea, Bharatpur Beach, and complete tranquility.
Ross Island Ruins
The abandoned British administrative capital — now reclaimed by jungle and deer. The ruined church, bakery, and tennis courts are haunting and atmospheric.
Scuba Diving
The Andamans is one of India's best scuba diving destinations — excellent visibility, rich marine life, and dive sites accessible even for beginners.

Travel Tips

Essential Andaman Islands Travel Tips

Ferry booking is critical
→The government ferry between Port Blair, Havelock, and Neil Island must be booked weeks in advance during peak season. We handle this as part of your package.
→Private speedboats are faster (1 hr vs 2.5 hrs to Havelock) but more expensive. We advise based on your budget.
→Don't leave ferry booking to chance — availability is genuinely limited and last-minute booking is often impossible.
What to bring
→Reef-safe sunscreen — the coral reefs are fragile and regular sunscreen damages them. This rule is enforced.
→A waterproof phone case or underwater camera — the marine life is extraordinary and you'll want to capture it.
→Cash — ATMs are limited beyond Port Blair. Withdraw enough for your entire Havelock and Neil stay.
→Light, quick-dry clothing — the humidity is high and laundry drying is slow.
Scuba & snorkelling
→Scuba certification courses available at Havelock — a 3-day Open Water PADI course is possible during your trip.
→Elephant Beach is the best snorkelling spot without scuba — book the boat through your hotel or tour manager.
→Snorkelling equipment is available to rent at most beach resorts on Havelock and Neil.
