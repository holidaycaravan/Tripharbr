About Mussoorie

The Queen of Hills

Mussoorie sits at 2,000m in the Garhwal Himalayas of Uttarakhand, 35km from Dehradun and a 6-hour drive from Delhi. The view from the western end of Mall Road — across the Doon Valley and into the snow-covered Himalayan ranges including Kedarnath and Bandarpunch — is one of the great views of North India, and on a clear October morning it is genuinely breathtaking.

The colonial-era Mall Road is Mussoorie's spine — lined with Victorian-era buildings, cafes, and shops. Camel's Back Road offers the best sunset views in the hill station. Kempty Falls (15km away) is one of the most visited natural attractions in the Himalayas. And the quiet cantonment village of Landour — beloved by author Ruskin Bond who has lived here for decades — is one of India's most charming and literary corners.

For families from Delhi, Mussoorie is the most accessible Himalayan hill station — 6 hours by road or via Dehradun. The Gun Hill ropeway gives families spectacular views without any trekking. And in winter (December through February), snowfall transforms Mussoorie into something magical. Tripharbr arranges everything — hotel, transfers, Dehradun pickup, and all activities.

What to Do

Top 6 Highlights of Mussoorie

Every experience is included or arrangeable in your Tripharbr Mussoorie package — no chasing vendors, no haggling

Mall Road & Camel's Back Road
Mussoorie's colonial promenade and the 3km walking road above it with the best sunset views in the hill station. The Himalayan snowline glows pink and gold at dusk.
Gun Hill Ropeway
A cable car to Mussoorie's highest point at 2,122m. 360-degree views of the Himalayan snowline, the Doon Valley, and the town below. Best on a clear morning.
Kempty Falls
A beautiful multi-tiered waterfall 15km from Mussoorie. Set in a forested gorge with a natural pool at the base. Most spectacular October through December.
Landour Village
The quiet cantonment neighbourhood where Ruskin Bond has lived and written for decades. Char Dukan cafe, charming bookshops carrying his work, and the best breakfast views in Mussoorie.
Dhanaulti Day Trip
A serene forest retreat 24km from Mussoorie. Dense cedar and rhododendron forests, echo point, and a completely different atmosphere from busy Mussoorie.
Mussoorie Heritage Walk
The colonial architecture of Library Chowk, Savoy Hotel (one of India's oldest), Christ Church, and the old cemetery tell the story of the hill station's extraordinary Victorian past.

Travel Tips

What You Need to Know Before You Go

Getting to Mussoorie
From Delhi by road: 6–7 hours via Dehradun on NH-334B. Most travelers depart by 5–6 AM and arrive by noon.
Fly to Dehradun's Jolly Grant Airport (1.5 hours from Delhi by air) then drive 58km to Mussoorie (1.5 hours).
Shatabdi Express from Delhi to Dehradun (5.5 hours) then taxi to Mussoorie (1.5 hours) — comfortable and scenic.
Landour — What to Do
Visit Char Dukan — four small shops that have been there since the British era. Best breakfast spot in Mussoorie with extraordinary views.
Ruskin Bond visits local bookshops occasionally — Cambridge Book Depot on Mall Road carries his complete works.
The Landour Language School (one of India's oldest Hindi language schools) is open to visitors for a brief tour if interested.
Best Time & Crowds
May and June are the most crowded months as North Indian families escape the summer heat. Book accommodation 2–3 months ahead for these months.
September and October offer the clearest Himalayan views of the year — the post-monsoon air is crystal clean. Our personal favourite months.
December visits can be magical if snowfall occurs but it is not guaranteed — Shimla and Kufri are more reliable for snow.
