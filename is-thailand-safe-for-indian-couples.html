Is Thailand Safe for Indian Couples and Honeymooners?

Everything Indian couples need to know before booking Thailand — safety, culture, scams, food, and what to expect.

Quick AnswerYes — Thailand is very safe for Indian couples. It is one of Asia's most visited tourist destinations with excellent infrastructure and a deeply tourism-oriented culture. The main risks are scams and traffic rather than physical safety. Thailand is welcoming of couples including unmarried couples, and Indian tourists are well-received throughout the country.

Is Thailand physically safe for Indian tourists?

Thailand has a very low rate of violent crime against tourists. The country’s economy depends heavily on tourism and the Thai government maintains strong safety standards in tourist areas. Bangkok, Chiang Mai, Phuket, Krabi, and Koh Samui — the main tourist destinations — are all well-policed and thoroughly safe for couples and families.

What scams should Indian couples watch out for in Thailand?

  • Tuk-tuk gem scam — a friendly driver offers a cheap sightseeing tour that ends at a gem store. Decline.
  • Temple closed scam — a stranger tells you a temple is closed and offers to take you somewhere else. Ignore — temples are rarely closed.
  • Taxi without meter — always insist on the meter in Bangkok taxis or use Grab app.
  • Overpriced ping pong shows — touts outside Patpong market invite tourists for a "free show" that comes with an enormous bill.
  • Beach jet ski damage scam — photograph the jet ski before renting. Get a written condition report.

Is Thailand respectful of Indian couples?

Thailand is one of Asia’s most tolerant and welcoming countries for couples. Public displays of affection in tourist areas are accepted. Unmarried couples face no issues staying together in hotels. Thai people are famously polite and welcoming to international visitors. Indian couples are a common and well-received demographic in Thailand.

What should Indian couples know about Thai culture?

  • Remove shoes before entering temples and homes
  • Dress modestly at temples — shoulders and knees should be covered (sarongs are often provided at temple entrances)
  • Do not touch anyone’s head — the head is considered sacred in Thai culture
  • Do not point feet at people or sacred objects — feet are considered the lowest part of the body
  • The Thai Royal Family is deeply revered — avoid any negative comments about the monarchy
  • Greet with a "wai" (palms pressed together with a slight bow) — Thais appreciate this gesture from visitors

Is Thailand safe for Indian women?

Thailand is generally considered safe for Indian women including solo travelers and couples. Tourist areas are well-lit and busy. Use Grab for transport, stay in reputable accommodation, and avoid poorly lit areas late at night. The Thai culture of "sanuk" (fun and friendliness) generally creates a welcoming environment for all visitors.

Is Thai food suitable for Indian vegetarians?

With some navigation, yes. Thai cuisine uses fish sauce (nam pla) as a base flavour in most dishes — similar to how Indian cooking uses salt. Always specify "no fish sauce, no meat" when ordering. Vegetarian-friendly dishes include: spring rolls (check the filling), fried rice with vegetables (specify no oyster sauce), papaya salad (specify no dried shrimp), and fresh tropical fruit. Buddhist vegetarian restaurants (marked with a yellow flag) serve fully plant-based food and are found throughout Thailand.

Is Thailand Right for Indian Couples?

Thailand is outstanding for Indian couples — beautiful islands, excellent food, fascinating temples, and a warmly welcoming culture. The Phi Phi Islands, the temples of Chiang Mai, and the Bangkok street food scene are among Asia’s greatest travel experiences. Go with basic scam awareness and you will have an extraordinary trip.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do Indians need a visa for Thailand?
As of 2024, Indians get 60 days visa-free entry to Thailand. No fee and no prior application needed.
What is the best time to visit Thailand from India?
November to April — dry season with excellent beach weather. December and January are peak season. February and March are ideal — good weather, fewer crowds than December.
Is Phuket or Krabi better for Indian couples?
Krabi for natural beauty and quieter beaches. Phuket for more facilities, nightlife, and easier access. Most couples enjoy 3 nights Phuket plus 2 nights Krabi for the best of both.
How long is the flight from India to Thailand?
4–5 hours from Delhi or Mumbai to Bangkok (Suvarnabhumi). Multiple direct flights daily on IndiGo, Air India, Thai Airways, and others.
Is Thailand more romantic than Bali for Indian honeymooners?
Bali edges Thailand for romance — private pool villas, rice terraces, and temple sunsets are hard to beat. Thailand wins on beaches and adventure. Choose Thailand if island-hopping and great food matter most; choose Bali if a romantic, intimate villa experience is the priority.
