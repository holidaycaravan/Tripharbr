Is the Maldives Really That Expensive for Indian Travelers?

The honest answer — with real numbers, not marketing fluff.

Quick AnswerThe Maldives is expensive — but not unaffordable. There is a wide range of options: local island guesthouses start at ₹8,000–₹15,000 per night per couple, while luxury overwater villas cost ₹80,000–₹2,00,000+ per night. A 5-night Maldives trip for a couple on a mid-range budget costs ₹1,20,000–₹1,80,000 total including flights. The key is choosing the right type of accommodation for your budget.

Why does the Maldives have a reputation for being expensive?

The Maldives’ luxury reputation comes from its world-famous private island resorts — Soneva Jani, Four Seasons, One&Only — where rooms cost USD 1,000–5,000 per night. These resorts are genuinely extraordinary but represent only one end of the spectrum. What most people don’t know is that the Maldives also has a thriving local island guesthouse scene that offers beautiful beaches, snorkelling, and the Maldives experience at a fraction of the cost.

What are the different types of Maldives accommodation?

There are 3 distinct categories:

  • Local Island Guesthouses (Budget–Mid): Guesthouses on inhabited local islands like Maafushi, Thoddoo, and Dhigurah. Room rates: USD 80–200 per night (₹6,500–₹16,500). You get a clean beach, snorkelling, and the Maldives experience. You do not get a private beach or overwater villa.
  • Mid-Range Resort Islands: 3-4 star resort islands with beach bungalows and good facilities. Room rates: USD 200–500 per night (₹16,500–₹41,500). Private beach, pool, water sports included.
  • Luxury Private Island Resorts: The famous overwater villas and private island experiences. Room rates: USD 600–5,000+ per night. The full Maldives fantasy — private pool, glass floor, butler service.

What does a realistic Maldives trip cost for Indians?

Here is a real breakdown for a couple, 5 nights:

Budget option (local island guesthouse):

  • Flights (Delhi–Male return): ₹25,000–₹40,000 per couple
  • Accommodation (5 nights): ₹40,000–₹60,000 per couple
  • Speedboat transfers: ₹8,000–₹12,000 per couple
  • Meals and activities: ₹15,000–₹20,000
  • Total: ₹88,000–₹1,32,000 per couple

Mid-range option (resort island):

  • Flights: ₹25,000–₹40,000
  • Accommodation (5 nights): ₹1,00,000–₹2,00,000
  • Transfers: ₹15,000–₹25,000
  • Meals: ₹20,000–₹30,000
  • Total: ₹1,60,000–₹2,95,000 per couple

Luxury option (overwater villa):

  • Flights: ₹30,000–₹50,000
  • Accommodation (5 nights): ₹4,00,000–₹10,00,000+
  • Everything else: included in all-inclusive
  • Total: ₹5,00,000–₹15,00,000+ per couple

Is the Maldives worth the cost compared to Andaman?

Andaman gives you a similar beach quality at roughly 30–40% of the Maldives cost — no international flights, no foreign exchange, no visa. For pure value, Andaman wins. But the Maldives delivers something Andaman cannot: the overwater villa experience, the turquoise atoll views from a seaplane, and the complete isolation of a private island. If budget is the priority, choose Andaman. If the experience is the priority and budget allows, the Maldives is worth every rupee.

How do you save money on a Maldives trip?

  • Book flights 3–4 months in advance — prices drop significantly
  • Travel in May or October — shoulder season, 20–30% cheaper than peak
  • Choose a local island guesthouse for your first trip
  • Book the seaplane in advance — last-minute seaplane seats are significantly more expensive
  • Use an all-inclusive plan if at a resort — food in the Maldives is expensive à la carte
Is the Maldives Worth It?

If a private beach, crystal-clear water, and a genuine tropical paradise experience is what you’re after — yes, the Maldives is worth it. Choose your accommodation category wisely, book in advance, and travel in shoulder season. The experience is unlike anywhere else in the world.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the cheapest way to do the Maldives from India?
Fly into Male on IndiGo or Air India (cheapest routes), stay on a local island like Maafushi, and take day trips for snorkelling and excursions. Total cost for a couple for 5 nights can be as low as ₹90,000–₹1,20,000.
Do Indians need a visa for the Maldives?
No. Indians get a free 30-day visa on arrival at Velana International Airport in Male.
How long is the flight from India to Maldives?
2.5 hours from Mumbai, 3.5 hours from Delhi. Short and direct on most major airlines.
Is the Maldives good for non-swimmers?
Yes. Snorkelling with a life jacket or vest is available at all resorts and guesthouses. You do not need to know how to swim to enjoy the Maldives water experiences.
What is the best time to visit the Maldives from India?
November to April is the dry season — best weather and clearest water. January and February are the peak months. May and October offer good deals in shoulder season.
