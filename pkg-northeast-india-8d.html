Day-by-Day Itinerary
Day
1
Arrive Guwahati, Assam
Airport pickup · Kamakhya Temple · Umananda Island · Brahmaputra sunset
▼
Fly into Guwahati — gateway to the Northeast. Your tour manager meets you and takes you directly to the hilltop Kamakhya Temple — one of India's most sacred Shakti temples, with sweeping Brahmaputra views. Ferry to tiny Umananda Island temple on the river. Sunset on the Brahmaputra ghats — the river is impossibly wide.
Transfer Kamakhya Temple Brahmaputra Dinner
Day
2
Kaziranga National Park (Jeep Safari)
Morning jeep safari · One-horned rhinos · Elephants · Wild buffalo
▼
Drive 4 hours to Kaziranga National Park — the UNESCO World Heritage site that saved the Indian one-horned rhinoceros from extinction. Morning jeep safari through the Central Range: rhinos grazing at close range, wild water buffalo, swamp deer, and if lucky, tigers and elephants. Afternoon elephant back safari for a different, quieter perspective.
Rhino Jeep Safari Elephant Safari Transfer Breakfast
Day
3
Majuli River Island
Ferry crossing · World's largest river island · Sattra monasteries
▼
Ferry from the north bank of the Brahmaputra to Majuli — the world's largest river island (875 sq km), home to the Vaishnavite Sattra monasteries that preserved Assamese culture through centuries. Cycle between the sattras on bamboo bicycles. Watch mask-making and Sattriya dance rehearsals. A day outside of time.
Ferry Majuli Island Sattra Visit Breakfast
Day
4
Majuli → Tezpur → Tawang (begins)
Drive north into Arunachal Pradesh · Bhalukpong · Forest checkpoint
▼
Drive north from Majuli back to the mainland and begin the ascent into Arunachal Pradesh. Enter via Bhalukpong — the Inner Line Permit checkpoint. Drive through the stunning Kameng River valley, the landscape turning from plains to jungle to pine-clad hills. Overnight at Dirang — a charming valley town with a dzong and hot springs.
Transfer (Tezpur–Dirang) Bhalukpong Checkpoint Breakfast + Dinner
Day
5
Dirang → Tawang
Sela Pass (13,700ft) · Sela Lake · Jung Falls · Tawang arrival
▼
The journey over Sela Pass (13,700 feet) — snow-flanked in winter, wild flowers in summer, always dramatic. Sela Lake at the top: turquoise water at altitude, prayer flags, and yaks. Jung Falls cascading 100 feet in multiple streams. Arrive Tawang — the 400-year-old Buddhist monastery town at 10,000 feet, the birthplace of the 6th Dalai Lama.
Transfer via Sela Pass Sela Pass Tawang Monastery Breakfast + Dinner
Day
6
Tawang Monastery & Bumla Pass
Tawang Gompa · Bumla Pass (15,200ft) · Shungatser Lake · Craft bazaar
▼
Tawang Monastery — the largest Buddhist monastery in India and second largest in Asia, founded in 1681. The main hall's 28-foot golden Buddha is staggering. Drive to Bumla Pass (15,200 feet) on the India-China border — one of the highest motorable passes in the world. Shungatser Lake, also called Madhuri Lake after the filming of a Bollywood song there. Evening at the Tawang bazaar for local weaves and thangkas.
Tawang Gompa Bumla Pass Shungatser Lake Breakfast
Day
7
Return: Tawang → Shillong (Meghalaya)
Scenic descent · Enter Meghalaya · Mawlynnong · Dawki river
▼
Descend from Tawang and drive south through Assam to Meghalaya. Arrive at Mawlynnong — Asia's cleanest village. Then the unmissable Dawki River: board a wooden boat on water so transparent it appears you're floating on air above the riverbed. Overnight in Shillong.
Transfer (Tawang–Shillong) Mawlynnong Dawki River Breakfast
Day
8
Shillong & Departure from Guwahati
Living Root Bridge · Elephant Falls · Guwahati airport drop
▼
Morning at the Nongriat Living Root Bridge (short trek) or Elephant Falls if time is tight. Drive back to Guwahati airport for your flight home — eight days through a part of India that most Indians have never seen. Return with the kind of stories that make you the most interesting person at any dinner party.
Root Bridge Elephant Falls Transfer (Guwahati Airport) Breakfast
