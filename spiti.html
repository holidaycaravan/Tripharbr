About Spiti

The Hidden Valley That Changes You

Spiti Valley is often described as being between India and Tibet — culturally, geographically, and spiritually. Tucked deep in the trans-Himalayan region of Himachal Pradesh, it sits at an average altitude of 3,800 metres, cut off from the rest of India by snow for seven months of the year. When the roads open in June, Spiti emerges like a secret kept by the mountains.

The landscape is unlike anything else in India. Bone-dry and lunar, with rivers of impossible blue running through steep canyons of orange and grey rock. Ancient Buddhist monasteries cling to cliff faces. Villages of flat-roofed stone houses dot the valley floor. The air is so thin and clean it tastes different. And the silence — especially at night when the Milky Way fills the entire sky — is total.

Spiti is best for groups of friends, adventure couples, and anyone who wants an experience that feels genuinely remote without being unnecessarily dangerous. We take care of all logistics — vehicles, stays, permits, route planning — so you experience the wildness without the stress.

What to Do

Top 6 Experiences in Spiti Valley

Key Monastery
Explore Spiti's most famous monastery perched at 4,166m. Monks in crimson robes, butter lamps, ancient thangka paintings, and panoramic views of the entire Spiti Valley floor far below.
4,166m · 14km from Kaza
Chandratal Lake Trek
The Moon Lake — a crescent of brilliant turquoise water at 4,300m ringed by snow peaks. The 4km trail from base camp to the lake and back is one of the most beautiful short treks in India.
4,300m · 2 hrs from Kaza
Stargazing at Kaza
At 3,800m with zero light pollution, Spiti's night sky is one of the finest astronomical experiences available to travelers anywhere. The Milky Way is so bright it casts shadows.
Best: Jul–Sep · Every night
Homestay in Kibber
Stay with a local family in Kibber — one of the world's highest inhabited villages. Wake to views of the Spiti River far below, eat traditional Spitian food, and experience a culture completely unchanged for centuries.
Kibber · 4,270m altitude
Dhankar Monastery & Lake
A 1,000-year-old monastery dramatically perched on a cliff above the confluence of two rivers. Trek 45 minutes above it to a small high-altitude lake that reflects the sky perfectly.
80km from Kaza
Hikkim — World's Highest Post Office
At 4,400m, Hikkim has the world's highest functional post office. Send a stamped postcard home — it actually arrives. A quirky, memorable thing to do at the top of the world.
4,400m · Near Kaza

Best Time to Visit Spiti — Month by Month

Spiti is only open June to October. The window is short — plan early.

January
Closed
Deep snow. Roads completely cut off. Temperature -20°C to -30°C. Inaccessible.
February
Closed
Still completely frozen. Locals are isolated. Snow Leopard Festival (rare local event).

Closed
Roads still blocked by snow. Only accessible to very experienced mountaineers.

Roads slowly clearing. Some stretches open by late May. Still risky — for experienced only.

Travel Tips

Essential Spiti Valley Travel Tips

Altitude Acclimatisation
Spiti averages 3,800m with many spots above 4,000m. Acclimatisation is non-negotiable.
Spend 2 nights in Shimla or Manali before entering Spiti — crucial for acclimatisation
Do not fly directly into Kullu and immediately drive to Spiti
Carry Diamox — consult your doctor before travel
Headache and nausea are normal in the first 24 hours — rest immediately
Roads & Vehicles
Spiti roads are among the most challenging in India. This is not an exaggeration.
4WD vehicles are mandatory — we provide Innova Crysta, Scorpio, or Bolero
Rohtang Pass (on Manali–Spiti route) requires a permit — we arrange it
Average driving speed in Spiti is 20–30 km/hr — distances take much longer than expected
River crossings are common on remote routes — only possible in right season and vehicle
What to Pack
Spiti temperatures can drop below 0°C even in summer nights. Pack accordingly.
Heavy thermals, fleece, and a good down jacket — always, even in July
SPF 60+ sunscreen — UV at altitude is intense and skin burns fast
Warm sleeping bag if camping at Chandratal (below 0°C at night)
Cash — there are no ATMs beyond Kaza. Carry enough for the entire trip
Connectivity & Essentials
Spiti is very remote. Prepare for minimal connectivity and basic facilities.
BSNL SIM is the only network with limited coverage in Kaza and main villages
No ATMs beyond Kaza — withdraw enough cash before entering the valley
Electricity is available but unreliable — charge everything when you can
Nearest hospital is in Reckong Peo (Kinnaur) — we carry a first aid kit on all trips
