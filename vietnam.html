About Vietnam

From Ha Long Bay to Hội An — A Country That Earns Its Reputation

Vietnam stretches 1,650km from north to south — an S-shaped country that manages to pack in extraordinary diversity. The north is dominated by Hanoi, one of Asia's most atmospheric capitals, and Ha Long Bay — 1,969 limestone islands rising from the Gulf of Tonkin in formations that look almost computer-generated. An overnight cruise here, waking up in the middle of the bay as mist clears from the karsts, is one of the great travel experiences of the world.

Central Vietnam holds the finest UNESCO sites: Hội An Ancient Town, a perfectly preserved trading port where Japanese merchant houses stand beside Chinese temples and French colonial facades. Lanterns drift on the river at night. The old city is closed to traffic. It is, without exaggeration, one of the most beautiful towns in Asia. Nearby Hue contains the Imperial Citadel — Vietnam's Forbidden City — and a valley of royal tombs spread across forested hills.

The south is Ho Chi Minh City — a megacity of motorcycles and phenomenal street food, where War Remnants Museum visits leave you thoughtful, and the Mekong Delta opens up just an hour away. Phong Nha in central Vietnam is home to the world's largest cave system, with Son Doong Cave — recently opened to trekkers — being a bucket-list experience unlike anything else on earth.

Top Highlights
Ha Long Bay Cruise — Overnight on a traditional junk boat among 1,969 limestone karsts. Kayaking into sea caves. Sundowners on deck as the light turns golden.
Hội An Ancient Town (UNESCO) — The world's most intact trading port. Lantern-lit evenings, tailor-made clothes, and food so good you'll eat the same dish twice.
Hanoi Old Quarter — 36 streets, each named for its trade. Egg coffee, pho at 6am, and the chaos of one of Asia's most liveable capitals.
Phong Nha Caves — The world's largest cave system, including Paradise Cave — 31km long, with chambers tall enough to contain a 40-storey building.
Hue Imperial Citadel — Vietnam's last royal capital. The Forbidden Purple City, royal tombs, and the Perfume River — hauntingly beautiful.
Vietnam Street Food Trail — Banh mi, pho, bun bo Hue, banh xeo, cao lau — every city has its own speciality. Vietnam's food is a destination in itself.

When to Go

Best Time to Visit Vietnam — Month by Month

Vietnam's climate varies significantly from north to south. The central coast (Hội An, Da Nang) has its own monsoon pattern — October–November brings typhoons there. Plan accordingly.

January
Peak Season
Dry and cool in the north (16°C in Hanoi). South is sunny and warm. One of the best months island-wide.
February
Peak Season
Tet (Lunar New Year) falls here — festive but many businesses close for a week. Book far in advance.
March
Best Month
Excellent across the country. Not too hot in the south. Hanoi warming up pleasantly. Highly recommended.
April
Best Month
One of Vietnam's finest months. Dry and warm everywhere. Ha Long Bay at its most beautiful.
May
Good
Heat building in the south. North remains pleasant. Central coast starts getting warm and sunny.
June
Hot & Wet
South monsoon season. HCM City gets afternoon downpours. Hanoi hot and humid. Beach areas busy.
July
Monsoon
Hottest and wettest month in south and central regions. Ha Long Bay can be choppy. Avoid if possible.
August
Monsoon
Typhoon risk for central coast. Heavy rain in south. North highlands (Sapa) beautiful and green.
September
Transitional
South monsoon easing. North getting drier. Good value, fewer tourists. Central coast still wet.
October
Good North
North and south excellent. Central Vietnam (Hội An, Hue) can receive typhoons — check before visiting.
November
Season Opens
North and south both good. Central coast clearing. Hanoi's best month — cool, dry, atmospheric.
December
Peak Season
Excellent throughout. Cool and dry in north, warm in south. Ha Long Bay calm. Festive atmosphere.
Travel Tips

What You Need to Know Before You Go

Visa & Entry
Indian nationals require an e-visa to enter Vietnam. It's a straightforward online process we handle for you as part of your Trip Harbour package.
Vietnam e-visa: apply at evisa.xuatnhapcanh.gov.vn — usually approved in 3 business days
Cost: approximately USD 25 per person, valid for 90 days, single or multiple entry
Passport must be valid for at least 6 months from intended entry date
We assist with the e-visa application as part of every Trip Harbour Vietnam package
What to Pack
Vietnam's climate varies hugely from north to south. Pack smartly for the regions you're visiting.
Light cottons for the south; a jacket and layers for Hanoi in winter (Dec–Feb, down to 16°C)
Modest clothing for temples and pagodas — knees and shoulders covered
Comfortable walking shoes — Hội An and Hanoi's Old Quarter involve lots of walking on uneven stone
A compact rain jacket is wise even in dry season — brief showers can happen any time
Getting Around
Vietnam is long — flying between Hanoi and Ho Chi Minh City is the most time-efficient approach. We arrange all internal logistics.
Fly into Hanoi or Ho Chi Minh City — direct flights from Delhi, Mumbai, Chennai and Bangalore
We arrange internal flights where needed and private transfers between cities
Grab taxis in cities are excellent — download the app for safe, metered rides
The scenic Reunification Express train (Hanoi–Da Nang) is bookable as an add-on with us
Money & Food
Vietnam is one of Asia's best-value destinations. The food is extraordinary — street food here is a genuine highlight, not an afterthought.
Currency is Vietnamese Dong (VND) — 1 INR ≈ 300 VND. Exchange at airports or ATMs
Must try: pho in Hanoi, banh mi in Hoi An, bun bo Hue, banh xeo (sizzling pancakes) in the south
Buy a Viettel or Mobifone SIM at the airport for cheap 4G data across the country
Vietnam is very safe for tourists — petty theft in busy areas is the main caution; use a money belt
