The Complete Kerala Travel Guide for Indians 2026

Backwaters, tea plantations, wildlife, beaches, and Ayurveda — the complete guide to planning a perfect Kerala trip.

Quick AnswerKerala is best visited October through March. Fly to Kochi or Thiruvananthapuram. The classic circuit covers Kochi (1 day), Munnar (2 days), Thekkady (1 day), Alleppey houseboat (1 night), and Kovalam beach (1 day) in 6–7 days. Budget ₹22,000–₹45,000 per person excluding flights. The houseboat is non-negotiable — it is Kerala’s most distinctive and memorable experience.

Why Kerala is called God's Own Country

Kerala is India's most consistently beautiful state — 600km of coastline, the Western Ghats running along its eastern border, and the unique backwater system of canals, lakes, and rivers in between. The state is also India's most literate and has among its highest human development indicators — which translates to excellent tourist infrastructure, honest service, and a level of care for visitors that distinguishes Kerala from most Indian destinations. The food is extraordinary, the landscape is lush year-round, and the culture — from Kathakali dance to snake boat races to the Theyyam ritual art form — is rich and distinctive.

When to visit Kerala

October–March (Best Season): The dry season when backwaters are calm, beaches are swimmable, hill stations are clear, and wildlife sightings are best. December and January are peak — book everything 6–8 weeks ahead. October and November are our top picks — post-monsoon freshness, fewer crowds, lower prices.

June–August (Monsoon): The famous Kerala monsoon — heavy rain, extraordinarily lush green landscape, dramatic waterfalls. Not ideal for beaches or houseboats but perfect for Ayurveda (the traditional treatment season) and a completely different kind of Kerala experience.

September: Onam festival — Kerala's most important celebration with snake boat races, floral arrangements, and the 26-dish Onam Sadya feast. A remarkable cultural experience if you time it right.

How to reach Kerala

By Air: Fly to Cochin International Airport (Kochi) — the most convenient arrival point for the classic circuit. Direct flights from Delhi (3 hours), Mumbai (2 hours), Bengaluru (1 hour), Chennai (1 hour). Also possible to fly into Thiruvananthapuram (for South Kerala) or Calicut/Kozhikode (for North Kerala).

By Train: Several overnight trains connect Delhi, Mumbai, and Bengaluru to Kerala. The journey from Delhi takes 36–40 hours — comfortable for those with time and preference for train travel.

The classic Kerala circuit (6–7 days)

  • Day 1: Arrive Kochi. Fort Kochi heritage walk — Dutch Palace, Jewish Synagogue, Chinese fishing nets, St. Francis Church. Kathakali performance in the evening.
  • Day 2: Kochi to Munnar (4 hours). Tea plantation walk, Eravikulam National Park (Nilgiri Tahr sightings), sunset at Top Station with views into Tamil Nadu.
  • Day 3: Munnar. Mattupetty Dam, Echo Point, Tea Museum. Full day in the hills.
  • Day 4: Munnar to Thekkady (3 hours). Periyar Wildlife Sanctuary boat ride — elephant, bison, and bird sightings. Spice plantation visit.
  • Day 5: Thekkady to Alleppey (3 hours). Board houseboat for overnight stay — lunch, tea, and dinner on board while cruising the backwaters.
  • Day 6: Alleppey to Kovalam (3 hours). Beach day — Lighthouse Beach, cliff views at Varkala (40 minutes away).
  • Day 7: Depart from Thiruvananthapuram airport (45 minutes from Kovalam).

The Kerala houseboat — complete guide

The houseboat (kettuvallam) is Kerala’s most iconic experience. Traditional wooden rice boats converted into floating bedrooms — usually 1–3 bedrooms, a dining area, a sun deck, and a kitchen. A crew of 2–3 people cooks, steers, and serves throughout your stay.

Duration: 1 night is standard and sufficient for most travelers. 2 nights for those who want to fully relax and explore deeper backwaters.

Route: Alleppey (Alappuzha) is the main base. Houseboats cruise through the Vembanad Lake and surrounding canals — passing villages, paddy fields, coconut groves, and the daily life of backwater communities.

Category: Category A (₹10,000–₹18,000 per night including all meals) is our recommendation — good food, clean AC rooms, experienced crew.

Meals: All meals included. Breakfast, lunch, tea, and dinner prepared fresh on board. Kerala fish curry, prawn dishes, and rice are the highlights.

What to eat in Kerala

Must try: Kerala fish curry with red rice and coconut oil — one of India’s great meals. Appam with stew — a lacy rice pancake with mild coconut milk curry. Puttu and kadala curry — cylindrical steamed rice cake with black chickpea curry. Prawn moilee — prawns in coconut milk sauce. Karimeen (pearl spot fish) — Kerala’s signature fish, best at a backwater restaurant. Payasam — Kerala’s rice and coconut milk dessert.

Vegetarians: Kerala is excellent for vegetarians — the Sadhya (traditional feast on banana leaf) is entirely vegetarian with 25+ dishes. Avial (mixed vegetables in coconut and yogurt) is outstanding.

Ayurveda in Kerala

Kerala is the home of authentic Ayurveda — the traditional Indian medical system of oil treatments, herbal medicines, and lifestyle practices. The monsoon season (June–August) is considered the ideal time for Ayurveda treatment as the cool, moist air opens pores and makes treatments more effective. Many Kerala resorts and dedicated Ayurveda centres offer packages from 3 days to 3 weeks. A basic couple's Ayurveda treatment package starts at ₹8,000–₹15,000 per day at a good centre.

How much does Kerala cost?

Budget per person, 6 days, excluding flights:

  • Budget: ₹12,000–₹18,000
  • Mid-range: ₹22,000–₹32,000
  • Luxury: ₹45,000–₹90,000+
  • Flights from Delhi: ₹6,000–₹12,000 return
  • Total per person (mid-range): ₹30,000–₹46,000
Is Kerala Worth Visiting?

Kerala consistently ranks among India's most satisfying travel destinations. The houseboat experience is genuinely unique — nothing in India replicates sleeping on a traditional wooden boat in the backwaters. The food is outstanding. The landscape is beautiful year-round. And the warmth and professionalism of Kerala's tourism industry makes the trip feel effortless. Every Indian should experience Kerala's backwaters at least once.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Kerala good for first-time travelers?
Excellent — Kerala is one of India's most tourist-friendly states with good infrastructure, honest operators, and exceptional hospitality. A great choice for first-time independent travelers.
Which is better — Alleppey or Kumarakom for houseboat?
Alleppey has more route options and better houseboat variety. Kumarakom is quieter and more exclusive. First-time visitors should base the houseboat at Alleppey.
Is Kerala worth visiting in monsoon?
For Ayurveda — yes. For beaches and houseboats — no. The monsoon landscape is extraordinarily lush but outdoor activities are limited.
What wildlife can you see in Kerala?
Periyar National Park (Thekkady) — elephants, bison, sambar deer, otters, and over 260 bird species. Silent Valley and Wayanad for more remote wildlife. Kerala has excellent birdwatching year-round.
Do you need a car in Kerala?
Yes — the tourist circuit spans 400km+ and public transport between destinations is slow. A private AC vehicle with driver is the standard and most comfortable way to cover Kerala. We arrange this as part of every package.
