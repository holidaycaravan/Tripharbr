About Thailand

The Country That Has Something for Everyone — and Does It Brilliantly

Thailand is one of the world's great travel destinations for a reason: it has mastered the art of offering extraordinary experiences at every budget level. A street food meal in Bangkok costs less than a chai at an Indian airport. A luxury beach resort in Koh Samui competes with the Maldives at a third of the price. A cooking class in Chiang Mai is one of the most memorable experiences a traveler can have.

For Indian travelers, Thailand hits a particularly perfect note. It's close (5-6 hours from Delhi), vegetarian food is widely available, the culture is deeply spiritual and Buddhist, the people are extraordinarily friendly, and the value for money is remarkable. A Thailand honeymoon at ₹35,000 per person feels like a ₹1 lakh holiday — the resorts, the beaches, the food, the experiences are genuinely world-class.

Thailand works beautifully as a multi-city trip — Bangkok for culture and food, then fly to Phuket or Krabi for beaches, optionally adding Chiang Mai for nature and temples. Our packages are built around who you are: romantic and slow for honeymooners, fun and activity-filled for families, and unique and memorable for corporate teams.

Thailand's 4 Must-Visit Cities

Each city offers a completely different Thailand experience — we build your itinerary around the right combination for you

Bangkok
Culture · Food · Temples
The chaotic, brilliant capital. Grand Palace, street food, rooftop bars, floating markets, and the best shopping in Southeast Asia. Essential 2–3 days.
Phuket
Beaches · Nightlife · Resorts
Thailand's largest island — Patong Beach, Kata Beach, boat trips to Phi Phi, James Bond Island. Best for couples and beach lovers. 3–4 days.
Krabi / Railay
Beaches · Karsts · Adventure
Arguably Asia's most dramatic coastline. Railay Beach (only by boat), kayaking through caves, snorkelling at Koh Lanta. 2–3 days.
Chiang Mai
Temples · Elephants · Nature
Northern Thailand's cultural capital. Elephant sanctuaries, Doi Inthanon national park, 300+ temples, night bazaar, Thai cooking classes. 2–3 days.

When to Go

Best Time to Visit Thailand — Month by Month

Thailand has distinct wet and dry seasons. November to April is the sweet spot for most travelers.

January
Peak Season
Coolest and driest month. Perfect weather everywhere. Highly recommended but book early.
February
Peak Season
Excellent beach weather. Less crowded than January. Chiang Mai flower festival. Great value.
March
Ideal
Still dry, slightly warmer. Perfect for beaches and temples. Getting busy — book ahead.
April
Good
Hot but dry. Songkran Water Festival (mid-April) — Thailand's most famous celebration. Fun but chaotic.
May
Monsoon Starts
Southwest monsoon arrives. Rains begin. Phuket and Krabi get very wet. Not ideal for beaches.
June
Wet Season
Heavy rains on Andaman coast. Bangkok and Chiang Mai still manageable. Not for beach trips.
July
Wet Season
Peak monsoon on west coast. Eastern Gulf islands (Koh Samui, Koh Phangan) are actually good now.
August
Wet Season
Heavy rains. Best for Koh Samui (east coast). Budget travel — prices very low.
September
Transition
Rains reducing. Lush and green. Fewer tourists. Good for Bangkok culture and Chiang Mai.
October
Shoulder Season
End of wet season. Loy Krathong (lantern festival) — one of Thailand's most beautiful events.
November
Season Opens
Dry season returns. Perfect weather begins. Yi Peng lantern festival in Chiang Mai. Ideal start.
December
Peak Season
Christmas and New Year in Thailand — beach resorts, Bangkok parties, Chiang Mai festivals. Book very early.
Travel Tips

Essential Thailand Travel Tips for Indians

Visa on Arrival — How it Works
Indians get a free 30-day visa on arrival at Bangkok, Phuket, and Chiang Mai airports.
Have a return/onward ticket — immigration will ask for proof
Passport valid for 6+ months from travel date — non-negotiable
Carry THB 10,000 or equivalent as proof of funds (rarely checked but required)
We provide a complete pre-departure checklist for smooth entry
Cultural Etiquette
Thailand is deeply Buddhist. Respecting local customs ensures a warm welcome everywhere you go.
Never touch a Thai person's head — the head is considered sacred
Don't point feet at people or Buddha images — feet are considered the lowest body part
Dress modestly at temples — cover shoulders and knees
The Royal Family is deeply revered — avoid any negative comments
Getting Around
Thailand is easy to navigate between cities. We handle all transport in your package.
Bangkok to Phuket — 1.5 hr flight (we book this). Not worth taking the bus
Bangkok to Chiang Mai — 1 hr flight or overnight train (scenic but 12 hrs)
Within cities: Grab app (SE Asia's Uber) is safe and cheap — download before you go
Tuk tuks are for fun, not transport — always agree on price before getting in
Food & Money
Thailand is exceptional value. You can eat brilliantly for ₹150–300 per person at street stalls.
Most Indian vegetarians do fine — tofu dishes, veggie pad thai, fruit everywhere
Exchange THB at authorised counters — SuperRich (Bangkok) gives best rates
Street food from busy stalls is safe — the busier the stall, the fresher the food
Always carry small denomination THB — many street vendors don't give change for large notes
