About Sri Lanka

Eight UNESCO Sites. One Compact Island.

Sri Lanka is one of the most rewarding destinations in Asia — a teardrop-shaped island that packs extraordinary diversity into a country smaller than many Indian states. The ancient Cultural Triangle in the north-centre contains five UNESCO sites within easy driving distance: Sigiriya's sky fortress, Dambulla's painted cave temples, the sacred city of Anuradhapura, Polonnaruwa's ruins, and the royal city of Kandy where the Temple of the Sacred Tooth Relic draws pilgrims from across the Buddhist world.

The south of the island is a different world — Galle Fort's Dutch colonial streets and the whale-watching waters off Mirissa, where blue whales and sperm whales surface regularly from November to April. In the central highlands, Nuwara Eliya's tea country is a landscape out of a dream: emerald estates climbing misty hills, colonial bungalows, and some of the finest tea in the world, picked and processed before your eyes.

Yala National Park gives Sri Lanka one of the world's highest leopard densities per square kilometre — a safari here rivals anything in East Africa in terms of big cat sightings. Sri Lanka is genuinely superb for honeymooners, families, culture lovers, wildlife enthusiasts, and anyone who wants to combine beach, heritage, and nature in a single trip.

Top Highlights
Sigiriya Rock Fortress — Climb a 5th-century rock citadel rising 200m above the jungle. Frescoes, mirror wall, and views that go on forever.
Dambulla Cave Temple — Sri Lanka's largest cave temple complex, with five caves full of murals and over 150 Buddha statues, all carved into living rock.
Kandy & Temple of the Tooth — Sri Lanka's hill capital and the most revered Buddhist site in the country, home to a tooth relic of the Buddha.
Yala National Park — World's highest density of leopards. Also home to elephants, sloth bears, crocodiles, and over 200 bird species.
Mirissa Whale Watching — Blue whales, sperm whales, and spinner dolphins off the south coast from November to April. Nothing quite like it in South Asia.
Nuwara Eliya Tea Country — Misty highland estates producing world-famous Ceylon tea. Stay in a planter's bungalow and watch the sunrise over the hills.

What to Do

Top 6 Experiences in Sri Lanka

Every experience is included or arrangeable in your Trip Harbour Sri Lanka package — seamlessly handled, zero hassle.

Sigiriya Rock Fortress
Climb the ancient 5th-century rock citadel — 1,200 steps to a palace perched 200m above the jungle canopy. The frescoes of the celestial maidens and the Lion's Paw staircase are unforgettable.
Cultural Triangle · Half Day
Kandy Temple of the Tooth
Sri Lanka's most sacred shrine holds a tooth relic of the Buddha. Experience the evening Puja ceremony — drums, incense, and the glow of oil lamps in the gilded inner sanctum. Deeply moving.
Kandy · 2–3 hrs
Yala National Park Safari
Dawn jeep safari through Yala — world-renowned for its leopard density. Spot sloth bears, wild elephants, crocodiles, and painted storks in a single unforgettable morning drive.
Southern Sri Lanka · Full Day
Whale Watching — Mirissa
Board a sunrise boat off Mirissa to witness blue whales — the largest animals ever to have lived on Earth — surfacing just metres away. Spinner dolphins often join the boat ride out.
Mirissa · Nov–Apr
Tea Estate — Nuwara Eliya
Walk through rolling tea fields at 2,000m altitude. Watch skilled pluckers at work. Tour the processing factory to understand how a leaf becomes Ceylon tea, then taste fresh brew at the source.
Hill Country · Half Day
Galle Fort
Walk the ramparts of this 17th-century Dutch fort at sunset — boutique cafes, art galleries, and colonial architecture inside UNESCO-listed walls. One of the most atmospheric places in all of Asia.
Galle · Southern Coast

When to Go

Best Time to Visit Sri Lanka — Month by Month

Sri Lanka has two monsoon seasons affecting different coasts. The west and south coast (most popular) is best Nov–Apr. The east coast is best May–Sep.

January
Peak Season
Dry and sunny on west & south coast. Best beach weather. Whale watching excellent off Mirissa.
February
Peak Season
Ideal weather island-wide. Blue whale season in full swing. Crowds at their highest — book early.
March
Best Month
Excellent conditions. Heat builds gently. Whale watching continues. Great for the cultural circuit.
April
Good
Sinhala New Year celebrations (April 13–14). Warm with occasional showers starting towards end of month.
May
Monsoon Starts
Southwest monsoon arrives. West and south coast turn wet. East coast begins to open up.
June
Monsoon
Wet season on west/south coast. East coast (Trincomalee, Arugam Bay) is now excellent.
July
Monsoon
West and south remain wet. East coast at its peak — beautiful clear waters for diving and surfing.
August
Monsoon
Cultural Triangle and central highlands relatively dry. Kandy Esala Perahera festival — spectacular.
September
Transitional
Monsoon easing on west coast. Still excellent on east coast. Fewer tourists, great value.
October
Transitional
Short northeast monsoon brings occasional rain across the island. Prices low. Landscapes lush.
November
Season Opens
West and south coast clearing. Whale watching season begins. A wonderful time to visit — fresh and green.
December
Peak Season
Christmas and New Year peak. West coast beautiful. Whale watching in full swing. Book months ahead.
Travel Tips

What You Need to Know Before You Go

Visa & Entry
Indian nationals require an Electronic Travel Authorisation (ETA) to enter Sri Lanka. We assist with this as part of your package.
ETA can be obtained online at eta.gov.lk — usually instant approval
Cost: approximately USD 35 per person (single entry, 30 days)
Passport must be valid for at least 6 months from date of entry
We handle ETA application as part of your Trip Harbour package
What to Pack
Sri Lanka is tropical — light clothing works for the coast, but bring layers for the highland tea country which can be cool.
Light cotton clothing for coastal areas — it's hot and humid year-round
A light jacket or fleece for Nuwara Eliya and hill country (15°C at night)
Modest clothing for temple visits — shoulders and knees must be covered
Sunscreen, insect repellent, and a refillable water bottle are essential
Getting Around
Sri Lanka is best explored by private car/van. Distances between sites are manageable — the island is only 435km long.
Fly into Colombo (CMB) — direct flights from most Indian metros in 2–3 hours
We provide an air-conditioned vehicle and experienced driver-guide throughout
The Kandy–Nuwara Eliya scenic train ride is one of Asia's most beautiful — we include it
Tuk-tuks are ideal for short distances within towns and are fun to hail
Money & Connectivity
Sri Lanka's currency is the Sri Lankan Rupee (LKR). USD and INR are widely exchangeable. 4G connectivity is good in most tourist areas.
Exchange INR to LKR at the airport or licensed exchange counters — avoid street exchanges
Purchase a local SIM (Dialog or Mobitel) at Colombo airport for 4G data — around LKR 500
Credit cards accepted at most hotels and large restaurants; carry cash for local markets
Tipping is appreciated but not mandatory — 10% at restaurants is a fair norm
